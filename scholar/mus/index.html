Music

MUS coursework looks at music as cultural artifact and aesthetic system. Rock as a tradition reads like a long argument about authenticity, influence, and craft — the same conversations show up in visual design, game audio, and broadcast production. Useful vocabulary for talking about taste without hiding behind it.

  1. MUS 103 W10 Final Mar 15, 2025

    Legends of Rock: Michael Jackson and Bob Dylan

    A paired study of two artists whose careers persist across decades and genre shifts. Two tracks each, spaced about two decades apart, examined for what they reveal about authentic artistic evolution against the backdrop of cultural shift, new technology, and industry pressure.

  2. MUS 103 W08 Discussion Feb 23, 2025

    The Emergence of Punk: A Reaction to the Late 1970s Mainstream

    A short comparative on how punk manifested in the U.S. and U.K. in the second half of the 1970s, written for the W08 discussion in MUS 103. The frame is punk as a reaction against the highly produced, investor-shaped mainstream rock of the AOR era, rather than as a clean next step out of a parent genre.
