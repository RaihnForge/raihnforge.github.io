Triple Constraint Overview

Scope

A website targeting individuals and organizations in Kittitas County for:

  • Information on resources available for employment, food bank, childcare, unemployment benefits, COVID testing and vaccine schedule, and school updates on reopening.
  • A resources page for organizations on various agencies and companies.
  • A wellness page for uplifting the soul of people affected by COVID.

Our team is progressing steadily on achieving the final deliverable within time and budget directives. Distributing tasks to people with experience, skill, and passion in related areas has helped us maintain a high quality rating, and communication and accountability have deterred negative scope creep. We pivoted on the scope directive of humor. After consultation, humor was determined to be slightly out of scope. It was not removed but folded into a more cohesive and on-brand directive of Wellness, which includes humor, meditation, and exercise tip articles and resources. We are confident this satisfies stakeholders currently.

Time

The team has evaluated our critical path and maintained the schedule for the associated tasks. External influences on the project have been the most difficult challenges to overcome. Our management staff and team are part-time, and we are working in extraordinary circumstances. The global pandemic precipitated the necessity to reduce social contact. The impact on the project is that development and management must all be done remotely, which can cause challenges in production and communication. The communication element especially can require more time than otherwise anticipated. Consulting team members is a concerted effort and can require more dedicated time allotments.

Quality Management Plan

Purpose

The Project Quality Management Plan documents the necessary information required to effectively manage project quality from project planning to delivery. It defines a project’s quality policies, procedures, criteria for and areas of application, and roles, responsibilities, and authorities. The plan is created during the Planning Phase. Its intended audience is the project manager, project team, project sponsor, and any senior leaders whose support is needed to carry out the plan.

Organization, Responsibilities, and Interfaces

The first step in quality management is to define quality. The project manager and team must determine which quality criteria will be used in the project, looking at what donors, recipients, organizations, and other main stakeholders bring to that definition. In certain cases, the organization or the field may have standard definitions of quality the project can adopt.

Quality Planning

In creating this reference guide, the most important factor is the COVID-19 sources and information relevance. The first measure of our quality planning is the relevance of information on the website. Given the broad spectrum of areas, we divided the team accordingly to investigate and research those areas. Works cited and references were critical throughout the research process to ensure validity of findings.

The quality plan also identifies the requirements that the resources and products must provide in order to satisfy the needs and desires of the project stakeholders, describes the circumstances or conditions that render production below quality standards, and uses this knowledge to gain a shared understanding between the project team to help them define what is above and what is below quality.

Defining project quality. It should be the expectation of users to understand the information given and find it as a relevant tool that helps them against COVID-19. As this is a hypothetical project with no actual money being invested, there are no stakeholders gearing information toward one industry over another. This is open-source material delivered in a digestible manner, with no legal or government requirements to be considered, except for disclaiming that the product is intended purely to provide information and not to lobby for one service or organization over another.

Measuring project quality. Quality shall be treated at the same level as scope, schedule, and budget. If the project sponsor is not happy with the quality of the project’s results, the project team may need to change scope, schedule, and budget to fulfill the sponsor’s needs and expectations. To deliver on time and on budget when the scope is constrained, the project must build a good working relationship with all stakeholders and consider their defined or implied needs.

Quality Assurance

Quality Assurance is a mechanism to provide validation on the basis of evidence to ensure that donors, recipients, company managers, and other stakeholders fulfill the needs, standards, and specifications of the product. It ensures the presence and efficacy of process and process instruments, and the safeguards in place to ensure that the expected quality standards for the production of quality outputs are achieved.

Analyzing project quality. During the weekly meeting, each member makes sure that deliverables meet the criteria set by the Project Manager. Having met only a portion of the research and consistency of the updates, a dialogue was started involving the course instructor, who helped put the team on track and offered a new viewpoint on how to handle the deliverable.

Improving project quality. With input from the instructor, the team was able to delegate work more constructively and reframe what the outcome of the project was to look like and where to go from there. That result is reflected in this document in the forward-looking plan.

Quality Control

Quality Control is the use of strategies and practices that evaluate real quality output against expectations and determine appropriate action in response to a shortfall. It is a mechanism that monitors individual project outcomes to assess whether they comply with applicable requirements and identifies various approaches to resolve the causes of unsatisfactory results.

The aim of quality control is to improve quality. It includes reviewing project results to assess if they meet quality requirements or concepts based on project stakeholders’ expectations. Quality management also covers how the contractor handles the scope, budget, and timeline of the project.

In order for this team to comply with the steps it has put in place, a weekly meeting was set up to track speed and efficiency. As a result of these meetings, it was found several times that research and development had gone off track, and corrections were made within the team. To bring the final draft to its final level, a core group of non-team members will review and discuss the guide. Doing so should make sure that what has been collected is sensible and of benefit to a community that is excluded from the process of creation.

Risk Management Plan

Purpose

The risk management plan documents and records the procedures for managing risk throughout the project. It allows our team to think critically about what issues could arise, how likely they are, what degree of impact they may have if they occur, and how we will avoid these issues.

A risk is an event or condition that, if it occurs, could have a positive or negative effect on a project’s objectives. Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, responding to, monitoring, and reporting risks. This plan defines how risks associated with the COVID Uplift project will be identified, analyzed, and managed. It outlines how risk management activities will be performed, recorded, and monitored throughout the lifecycle of the project, and provides templates and practices for recording and prioritizing risks.

Qualitative Risk Analysis

Probability and impact for each identified risk are assessed by the project manager with input from the team.

Probability bands.

  • High: greater than 70% probability of occurrence.
  • Medium: between 30% and 70% probability of occurrence.
  • Low: below 30% probability of occurrence.

Impact bands.

  • High: risk that has the potential to greatly impact project cost, schedule, or performance.
  • Medium: risk that has the potential to slightly impact project cost, schedule, or performance.
  • Low: risk that has relatively little impact on cost, schedule, or performance.

Risks that fall within the red and yellow zones receive risk response planning, which may include both a mitigation and a contingency plan.

Risk Response Planning

Each major risk (those falling in the red and yellow zones) is assigned to a project team member for monitoring, so the risk does not fall through the cracks.

For each major risk, one of the following approaches is selected:

  • Avoid. Eliminate the threat by eliminating the cause.
  • Mitigate. Identify ways to reduce the probability or the impact of the risk.
  • Accept. Take no further action.
  • Transfer. Make another party responsible for the risk (insurance, outsourcing, etc.).
