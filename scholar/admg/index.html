Administration & Management

ADMG coursework at CWU covers the operational craft of running organizations and projects. The thread through these papers is the relationship between formal frameworks (Agile, Scrum, project management bodies of knowledge) and the messier reality of doing the work, where the principles are more useful than the prescriptions.

  1. ADMG 479 Final Project Plan Jun 5, 2025

    Agile Project Plan: Kelvin Content Initiative

    A full Agile project plan for a real initiative at Kelvin Education: producing eight five-part animated microlessons in Articulate RISE 360 and integrating them through KC3 (Kelvin Central Content Controller) into Kelvin Pulse. The plan walks the project-specific vision and success criteria, the interpersonal team dynamics, the product roadmap, the sprint planning, and worked examples of a daily stand-up and a sprint review plus retrospective.

  2. ADMG 479 M03 Apr 17, 2025

    Agile Process Terms

    A working definition of the ten core Agile roles and ceremonies, each annotated with the project manager's responsibility and the specific challenge inside that responsibility. Closes with a sketched-out lifecycle flowchart and a candid pick for the term that is hardest to do well.

  3. ADMG 479 M02 Apr 17, 2025

    Agile Manifesto Values and Principles

    An applied breakdown of the four Agile values and twelve principles, each grounded in real project experience at Kelvin Education rather than treated as a checklist.

  4. ADMG 285 M09 Final Mar 13, 2024

    Sustainability Proposal: A Life Cycle Assessment Study of Secondary-Use Cases for Cellular Devices

    A formal proposal to conduct a Life Cycle Assessment study testing whether donated, two-year-old iPhones can be repurposed into a productive secondary-use phase — communications augmentation, learning assistance, or workstation peripheral — instead of going to recycling or landfill. The proposal frames the work inside Circular Economy theory, Life Cycle Thinking, and the Triple Bottom Line, and lays out goal, scope, system boundaries, expected benefits, and the trade-offs honestly.

  5. ADMG 285 M08 Mar 10, 2024

    Annotated Bibliography: Sustainability LCA Study Proposal

    The annotated bibliography I built as the research scaffolding for the M09 Sustainability Proposal Final. Each entry lists the bibliographic citation, the key takeaway in my own words, and a short note on how I intended to use the source inside the final proposal. The bullet form is deliberate — it is a working researcher's document, not a formal essay, optimized for moving claims between drafts.

  6. ADMG 285 M05 Feb 19, 2024

    LCA Article Analysis: Modelling of Different Circular End-Of-Use Scenarios for Smartphones

    A structured analysis of the peer-reviewed journal article *Modelling of Different Circular End-Of-Use Scenarios for Smartphones* (Pamminger et al., 2021), summarized through the lens of ISO 14040-44 Life Cycle Assessment Study Design Parameters. The paper walks through the article's methods, system boundaries, functional unit, data sources, impact assessment, and concludes with my own recommendations for organizations adopting repair-first, refurbish-second, remanufacture-last strategies for cellular devices.

  7. ADMG 372 Final Dec 5, 2021

    Professional Development Plan & Leadership Philosophy

    The course-final synthesis paper for ADMG 372: a structured self-assessment (MBTI, Big 5/6, Emotional Intelligence), a SWOT analysis against real career constraints, a SMART goal plan from CWU graduation through 2030, and a ten-principle leadership philosophy grounded in the Marine Corps experience and the Portolese, Upperman, and Trumpy textbook.

  8. ADMG 372 M08 Nov 15, 2021

    Operational Leadership Considerations: Culture, Diversity, and the Top 50

    A three-part operational-leadership case study: organizational culture at Bungie (drawing on an informational interview with friends who worked there), a critique of the Central Washington University Veterans Center diversity policy as the embedded student worker, and an assessment of the DiversityInc Top 50 framework against the Portolese, Upperman, and Trumpy textbook.

  9. ADMG 374 Final Mar 15, 2021

    Final Individual Reflection: Team Project Performance

    End-of-quarter reflection on a four-person remote team that delivered a multi-lab nonprofit website project through the back half of COVID. Each teammate is graded with specific evidence, my own performance is graded against the Schwalbe 7 Habits, and the ten PMBOK knowledge areas are walked through against what the team actually did.

  10. ADMG 374 A08 Feb 27, 2021

    Communicating About and Managing Risk

    Two-chapter response paper covering project communication (tone, body language, virtual punctuation, distribution channels) and project risk (early identification, planned responses, and what a real risk management plan buys you). Each section pulls from Schwalbe and at least one outside reference.
