Administration & Management

ADMG coursework at CWU covers the operational craft of running organizations and projects. The thread through these papers is the relationship between formal frameworks (Agile, Scrum, project management bodies of knowledge) and the messier reality of doing the work, where the principles are more useful than the prescriptions.

  7. ADMG 285 M09 Final Mar 13, 2024

    Sustainability Proposal: A Life Cycle Assessment Study of Secondary-Use Cases for Cellular Devices

    A formal proposal to conduct a Life Cycle Assessment study testing whether donated, two-year-old iPhones can be repurposed into a productive secondary-use phase — communications augmentation, learning assistance, or workstation peripheral — instead of going to recycling or landfill.   9. ADMG 285 M7 Mar 4, 2024

  10. ADMG 285 M05 Feb 19, 2024

    LCA Article Analysis: Modelling of Different Circular End-Of-Use Scenarios for Smartphones

    A structured analysis of the peer-reviewed journal article *Modelling of Different Circular End-Of-Use Scenarios for Smartphones* (Pamminger et al., 2021), summarized through the lens of ISO 14040-44 Life Cycle Assessment Study Design Parameters.   21. ADMG 371 M9 Informational Interview Jun 8, 2021

    Informational Interview: Stakeholder Discovery as Career Research

    An applied research paper that treats the informational interview as a structured discovery method rather than a networking tactic. The first half synthesizes the academic literature (Bomzer, Doyle, Knight, Plakhotnik, Zhang, FiskeJul) on what an informational interview is, how it differs from a job interview, and what benefits a careful version produces. The second half walks the applied case study: organizational research on a major game studio in Bellevue, Washington, a six-category interview instrument built from the ADMG 371 framework, and a debrief on how the conversation reshaped my personal development plan. The interview subject has been generalized to a senior engineer at the studio to keep this archive public-safe; the methodology, citations, and findings are intact.

  22. ADMG 371 M2A Apr 8, 2021

    Management Theories: Scientific, Administrative, Behavioral, and the Contingency Frame

    A memo addressed to Dr. Susan Rivera walking the major schools of management theory. Opens with contingency theory and the disagreement between Fiedler's fixed-personality model and the Blanchard and Hersey flexible-leader model. Then surveys the concept and values of Scientific Management (Taylor's four principles, motion studies, factory orientation), Administrative Management (Fayol's fourteen principles and esprit de corps), and Behavioral Management (Mayo's attention, socialization, and meaning). Closes by naming Administrative Management as the recommended foundation, with Scientific and Behavioral Management used as augmenting pillars.
