About this paper

The course-final synthesis paper for ADMG 372: a structured self-assessment (MBTI, Big 5/6, Emotional Intelligence), a SWOT analysis against real career constraints, a SMART goal plan from CWU graduation through 2030, and a ten-principle leadership philosophy grounded in the Marine Corps experience and the Portolese, Upperman, and Trumpy textbook.

Why it matters to the practice

This paper is the closest thing I have to a written charter for how I actually try to lead. The ten principles (lead with competence, trust and communication, clear vision, awareness of power, integrity, integrated approach, lifelong learning, valuing others, ethical commitment, enriching others) still describe how I run the studio, mentor developers, and structure the Forge Framework. Re-reading it now, the goal table is half-met and half-revised, which is exactly what the reflection section predicted.

Connections

Foundational to the entire studio. Feeds directly into the Forge Framework's leadership chapter, the developer personality system in Facsimile (the MBTI, Big 5, and EQ scaffolding originates here), and the Keeper CTO model in the dev ecosystem. Introduction

Utilizing the knowledge gained through my experiences and academic endeavors thus far, I will conduct a leadership planning document in accordance with guidelines that are in keeping with the Four-Step Process in Leadership Planning. These include self-assessment, application of self-assessment, goal setting, and reflection (Portolese et al., 2018). Finally, leadership styles in terms of principles, values, and application will be addressed. These valuations will provide me clarity in personal growth, career pursuits, and application of leadership in order to affect positive change to individuals and community.

Application of Self-Assessment

SWOT Analysis

Strengths. My education and experiences are numerous, and all include working well with others and the leading of teams small and large. From Marine Corps combat environments to application development situations, I have proven to be effective at leading, motivating, and enriching groups, aligning myself and others to organizational mission, values, and vision while producing and achieving results. I exhibit traits of extraversion and charisma which afford me an affable disposition that boosts my soft skills. I tenaciously seek expert sources of power and influence in order to be responsible with choices and direction. I possess relatively high emotional intelligence and an MBTI personality type that excels at intuitive judgement and empathy, allowing for natural accumulation of referent power and influence. Technically I am apt at software and computer systems and accustomed to and effective in nearly any office environment.

Weaknesses. I am lacking in academic accreditation and experience. This limits my ability to communicate on academic terms and stifles my normally beneficial intuitive trait. I have some physical and mental conditions. I am in a challenging point in life that requires extra effort to recover from.

Opportunities. I have a great deal of experience leading and contributing to projects, which allows for the possibilities of new projects to present themselves. I have a great deal of networking capability in the gaming industry as well as with Veteran groups. My character of values, ethic, and authentic care of others affords me opportunities in possible positions for higher education administration.

Threats. Some physical and mental limitations exist and require consideration. Finances are not ideal for my age. My age may be a challenging factor in seeking career work post-graduation.

The Five Questions

Professions of Interest. I am interested in professions that will allow me to work with others as well as contribute to the enrichment of individuals, groups, organizations, and communities. I am extremely motivated by intrinsic rewards, while often uninterested in extrinsic rewards so long as I am able to maintain Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs (Portolese et al., 2018, p. 171).

External Limiting Factors. My disabilities including PTSD, back pain, and migraines potentially limit my geographic location and situation. Physical ailments make physical labor challenging, while migraines and anxiety often result in an overabundance of intense office work that may present itself in software development studios. Relocation is an option, but given my disabilities having a strong support structure is important. My past experience has proven that urban environments are not conducive to quelling my traumatic experiences and reactions.

Internal Limiting Factors. Recent life events have forced me to re-evaluate my world view of what is known and unknown. Re-gauging my capabilities and competencies has caused a lack of confidence. My internal angst has greatly impacted my emotional stability, and slightly dampened my openness to inquiry as well as my natural extraversion. Possessing both feeling and judging traits can at times clash and cause personal discord and disorganization. Challenging events in my personal life cause a lack of foundational confidence, and an overabundance of anxiety due to PTSD. My judgment trait can cause me to be too self-critical and negatively affect myself and others around me. This affects my normal level of impact and effectiveness in leading and contributing positively to teams if not kept in check. I am focused on shoring these factors up as much as is reasonably possible by executing several strategies of evaluation, counsel, and accountability on multiple fronts including personal, professional, and social.

Most Important in Work. The most important work attributes would be organizational mission, values, and vision that are aligned to my personal values: to utilize gifts and abilities to the edification of others, the individual, and the greater ethical good of all. I am interested in intrinsic rewards, and very much less in extrinsic rewards. Working in business and for-profit endeavors is a huge turn off for me. My experience serving others through the Marine Corps and other ventures has caused me great disillusionment with for-profit organizations. Work-life balance and family considerations are ideal, but not necessary for my personality.

Must Haves for Job. Having constant ability to utilize creative problem solving and continuous opportunity for improvement of self, others, and organization are musts. Being in a place where I would not be able to conduct positive change, add value, and add efficiency to processes and production would not be ideal for my personality. I value ethical conduct, aspiring purpose, and enrichment of self, community, and others.

Reflection

Initial planning is a necessary first step to greater personal growth, but it is the case that the journey will always be greater than any destination or goal achieved. Thus, it will be paramount to continuously reflect and modify the previous analysis and objectives. By continually applying lessons learned and interacting purposefully with mentors and career services, I will re-evaluate and learn from failures to continue to achieve successes in order to better myself and my community.

Leadership Philosophy

This section establishes organizational goals and leadership principles that will help assist my focus and conduct moving forward. I wish to continually re-evaluate and iterate on my leadership goals, values, and principles for as long as I am able.

Organization Goals

My goals for my organization are to extend my expertise and skills in order to honor and enrich those around me. I wish to be a person who continually provides any small or large assistance that may inspire hope in themselves and others. I wish to be courageous and ethical in my approach, and to wholistically and authentically engage with individuals and community. I am hoping to provide support for my fellow Veterans and make real changes in their lives that allow them to shine even brighter for their families, themselves, and the communities they touch.

Leadership Principles

Lead with Competence. Be informed while providing care, vision, and influence to motivate and orient a group to a common task and purpose.

Trust and Communication are Paramount. Be authentic and empathic, garnering trust and a caring atmosphere. Trust engenders honest and frequent communication to stay in touch with the health and needs of the organization.

Provide Clear Vision. Develop and commit to clear and often-communicated vision, mission, and values, the ethical foundations that relate to follower values in order to achieve organizational alignment and purpose.

Be Aware of Power. Understand elements of power, personal and positional, where it is and is lacking, and how and where it is being wielded to affect others.

Integrity in All Things. Successful teams must establish trust, harmony, commitment, accountability, and team focus. Modeling integrity provides the necessary foundation for these aspects.

Integrate Leadership Approach. Utilize power-influence and leverage situational approach aspects by understanding personal role and the need for adaptation based on the three Key Variables in Leadership.

Life-Long Learner. Ensure that referent power is managed and expert power is pursued, lest the negative aspects of charismatic leadership arise. Always understand what personal biases you may be bringing to the situation.

Value Others. Be diligent about respecting and empathizing with the individuals that comprise a group. Their rights, skills, values, and opinions, similar or different, are at least as valuable as your own, if not more.

Commit to Ethics. Maintain a strong foundation of personal values that are oriented to the consideration of societal issues. Avoid rationalizations, as they often are an indicator of unethical conduct.

Enrich Others. Model positive performance and accountability, while helping others achieve the best in themselves as well. The greatest gift is to assist another in finding the better parts of themselves.

Summary

Being an authentic and empathetic individual driven by ethics and values for the greater good of the community are my aspirations. I wish to utilize my skills, personality traits, and knowledge to the betterment of the world around me. To do this I must bear in mind that trust, harmony, commitment, accountability, and team focus are key to the success of any group, and my personal contributions should be considered and improved. It is my responsibility to improve the wonderous world I find myself in.
