About this paper

A mid-quarter status report written as a project-manager email to the professor, covering three personal SMART goals: a personal tasking and note system, a project management presentation process, and a modular maintainable resume. Includes a short critique of the assignment structure itself before the report begins.

Why it matters to the practice

Status reporting is the unglamorous communication skill that decides whether leadership keeps backing your work. Practicing it on my own goals, in my own real EdTech work environment, let me feel the difference between a stand-up update that lands and one that vanishes. The same template now drives how I report on Keyes Dev Studio backlog work and on the Forge Framework rollout.

Connections

Bridges into the agile work in ADMG 479 (status as a cadence ritual, not a one-shot artifact) and into the career-mngr project under the studio (the modular resume goal in this report is the same instinct that later became a tracked product). This is a mid-quarter status report on three personal SMART goals, written as a project-manager email to my instructor. Before the report itself I want to note something about the assignment structure, because part of the skill of professional communication is recognizing when the format gets in the way of the message.

A Note on the Assignment Format

A real project manager rarely updates on three unrelated goals in one email. The progress report template in the textbook is built for one project, with one set of milestones, one set of anticipated problems, and one infographic. Stacking three goals into a single template creates an abstraction layer that does not match how a supervisor actually digests progress. The cleaner version of this assignment is one report per goal, three reports total, in priority order. The cleaner version of the infographic is one per goal as well. I am submitting the combined version because that is the assignment, but I want to name the awkwardness on the record. Recognizing when the template is fighting the audience is part of the communication skill the class is teaching.

Professor Fountain,

Included are the results of my three submitted SMART goals for this quarter. The findings are formatted to the prescribed template, though I would request that future updates be split into each SMART goal or project individually in sequential priority order.

Background

My three previously submitted SMART goals orient toward improved professionalism in communication around daily upkeep, project status updates, and career planning, with the underlying intent of increasing confidence and self-value while improving opportunity engagement.

Personal Communicable Notation for Updates. Develop and execute a personal tasking and note management system that synergizes with my professional environment and role requirements by March 19, to increase confidence during work standup updates.

Presentation Process for Project Management. Communicate a project management approach through a repeatable presentation process inside a SaaS-based small organization, qualified through reception data, to improve my ability to communicate dense information by the end of the IT Communications course.

Modular Maintainable Resume. Create a maintainable and approachable resume-building process through modular components in Google Docs document tabs, reviewed for ease of use and effectiveness by the professor and document maintainer on a 100-point scale, to gain confidence in continuous engagement of my primary professional competency document.

Work Completed

Work on the three SMART goals has been varied, steady, and worthwhile. The goals are already making me a more effective professional communicator in the IT space.

Personal Communicable Notation for Updates is nearly complete. I trialed Trello, Basecamp, Jira, and Google Tasks against ease of use, connectivity to current work systems, and incurred cost. Google and Jira are the most interwoven in my work environment, but I believe Trello is the best long-term fit. It is suitably aligned with Jira at the company level, while being separable enough to own personally and to keep moving with me regardless of company dependency.

Presentation Process for Project Management is primarily complete. I have presented twice using my Canva slides process, once to my team and CTO and once to the CEO. The first round produced specific feedback that I rolled into the second round. The second presentation went very well, with expedited communication through summary and visualization, and nearly all proposed enhancements to our product were approved. One was approved in concept but deferred for current workload.

Modular Maintainable Resume is one phase away from completion. The modular resume is not yet consolidated into a single document, but the composite elements have been identified, updated, and gathered for consolidation while running a real-world job application process that uses every one of those elements.

Work To Be Completed

Most of the work is in hand. The remaining work is final refinement and a clean read on outcomes.

Personal Communicable Notation for Updates. The final phase runs over the next three weeks as I fully adopt Trello and unify my daily tasking around it. Once it is sufficiently ingrained I can give a detailed account of its relative impact and success.

Presentation Process for Project Management. While the goal itself is complete and successful, I want to refine the difference between proposal projects and progress-of-current-projects presentations, so the process travels cleanly across project types.

Modular Maintainable Resume. The final phase is to compile all resources into a single Google Doc with well-defined document tabs, so every future application can be generated from one updatable document.

Anticipated Problems

Problems are few, and would refine rather than block outcomes.

Personal Communicable Notation for Updates. Trello is the best fit, but there is a risk of duplication with work-side Jira tickets. I plan to link Jira tickets into the Trello board rather than re-document them, so the work system stays the source of truth and the personal board stays a routing layer.

Presentation Process for Project Management. The current potential issue is duplication confusion: a Confluence project plan, a Google Doc presentation outline, and a Canva deck. My iteration is to drop the Google Doc and use standard meeting notes to draft directly into the Canva deck.

Modular Maintainable Resume. Reference contact information may be better kept in a separate spreadsheet for location and contact updates. If so, I will maintain a formatted references sheet in the primary document and link to the contact database from there.
