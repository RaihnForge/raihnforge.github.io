Information Technology

IT coursework at CWU covers the technical foundations underneath modern production work, from hardware components and operating systems to information management and the practical decisions of speccing a workstation for the job. Papers here lean toward the applied side: how the concepts map to the kind of design and development I actually do.

  1. IT 490B Internship Final Jun 4, 2025

    ITAM Project Management Internship Reflection

    Capstone internship reflection covering Spring 2025 work at Kelvin Education as Lead Production Designer and Project Manager. Eleven sections walk learning objectives, organization, skills developed, field-experience benefits, self-analysis, advice received, AI as a tool, career development, problem solving, knowledge gained, and conclusion. Built on top of foundational experience from ITAM 490A and anchored in three frameworks I now use to actually run work: the project-management triple constraint, the ETTO principle, and Ikigai.

  2. IT 229 D06 Nov 17, 2024

    Basics of Computer Hardware Components

    A taxonomy of system software versus application software and an applied look at GPU and SMPS roles in a modern workstation, written from years of hands-on PC building and modification.

  3. IT 490A Internship Final Mar 13, 2024

    ITAM Internship A Reflection

    Winter 2024 capstone-internship reflection covering my first quarter at Kelvin Education, Inc. as the project manager embedded with the development and administration teams. Nine sections walk learning objectives, organization, skills, field-experience benefits, self-analysis, advice, suggested employer improvements, knowledge gained, and conflict resolution. This is the predecessor paper to IT 490B: the place where I first put Agile, Jira, SOC 2 Type 2, and remote leadership through real production, and where I first started naming the patterns (space and grace, double-loop learning, servant leadership) that 490B then refined into a working framework.

  4. IT 301 Final Essay Mar 10, 2023

    Can Closed Windows Be Open-Sourced?

    A research essay on Windows 11 telemetry, in-OS advertising, and the erosion of user choice, set against Valve's investment in open-source Linux gaming as a viable alternative. Written for IT 301 using Lessig's modalities of regulation.

  5. IT 468 A9 Mar 3, 2023

    Atomic Transactions, Rights Matrices, and Recovery: A James River Jewelry Case

    Assignment 9 for IT 468. A three-part database administration analysis of the James River Jewelry case. Part one decides which of three application transactions (Acceptance, Sales, Price Adjustment) must be made atomic to prevent lost updates, reasoning from logical units of work and multi-table modification. Part two designs processing rights for three user groups (Administrative Personnel, Managers, System Administrators) via a Database Rights Matrix that locks down the JEWELRY_ITEM.NegotiatedSalesPrice and JEWELRY_ITEM.CommissionPercentage columns. Part three critiques the company's current backup-to-manager's-house scheme and recommends cloud and isolated virtual server backups with encryption and proper recovery strategy.

  6. IT 301 Assignment 02 Jan 22, 2023

    Spokeo v. Robins: Data Broker Privacy and Concrete Harm

    An evaluation of Spokeo, Inc. v. Robins through Lessig's four modalities of regulation (Market, Law, Architecture, Norms). The case turns on whether misrepresented personal data published by a data broker constitutes 'concrete harm' under Article III standing, and what it means when the Supreme Court initially says no.

  7. IT 260 Final Project Dec 10, 2021

    Lark Resorts Software Solutions Plan

    Final executive summary for IT 260, written in the voice of a fictional resort organization's leadership recommending a Microsoft 365 ecosystem rollout to support a multi-property expansion. The piece had to act as a real document: an internal case for restructure, mapped to stakeholder needs and answered by a specific software stack.

  8. IT 165 M8A May 22, 2021

    Manually Segmenting Data

    A manager-facing memo that takes a raw sales spreadsheet, groups the columns into four operational categories (order identification, geographic location, shipping, and sales figures), and proposes the analyses each category unlocks. Includes a pivot table on average profit by region and year, a skeptical read of the data's integrity, and a documented list of actions taken on the worksheet.

  9. IT 238 W7 Feb 18, 2021

    Stuxnet and the Olympic Games: The First Kinetic Cyberweapon

    A study paper covering the Stuxnet worm (operation Olympic Games), its target on the Natanz nuclear facility in Iran, the technical anatomy of the four zero-day vulnerabilities it consumed, the man-in-the-middle subroutine that quietly destroyed centrifuges while feeding false telemetry to operators, and the strategic question of whether the United States should disclose zero-day vulnerabilities or hold them in reserve. Closes with a personal note connecting the material to my service in OIF and OEF and to the period when my wife was a linguist working classified missions at Fort Meade.
