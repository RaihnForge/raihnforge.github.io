About this paper

Week 03 weekly memo from my Spring 2025 ITAM Internship B at an EdTech employer. The assignment asked three things: describe the workplace technology stack and how it creates efficiency or savings, research three AI tools that could benefit the organization, and surface the best piece of advice received that week. Written six weeks before the capstone reflection, while I was deep in microlesson production and the Slack-to-Jira automation work.

Why it matters to the practice

This memo names the stack I was actually using daily as the acting Product Manager — Jira, Slack with Jira integration, Articulate Rise, CreateStudio 3 — and the AI tools I would have wanted to bring in if the organization had been ready: ChatGPT API for in-product support, Descript for narration revision, NLP platforms for open-ended survey analysis. The advice section also captures the moment the ETTO principle officially became my operating frame: 'progress beats perfection, ship then refine,' translated back to the efficiency-thoroughness trade-off I had picked up in ADMG 372. That frame went on to anchor the entire capstone.

1. Technology Usage

At the EdTech employer, technology plays a pivotal role in optimizing daily operations and enhancing workflow efficiency across teams. Three specific examples of tools and systems in use:

Jira (Atlassian)

Jira is the central tool for project tracking and task management. It enables our team to assign, prioritize, and monitor tasks within sprint cycles. This structure improves team coordination, reduces task redundancy, and increases transparency across cross-functional projects, especially during weekly sprint planning and retrospectives.

Slack with Jira Integration

We leverage Slack as a primary communication platform, but what enhances its effectiveness is the integration with Jira. Through this, we automate task creation directly from Slack conversations. This streamlines communication and ensures critical tasks are not lost in conversation threads, saving both time and effort in managing follow-ups.

Articulate Rise and CreateStudio 3

These content creation tools are used to develop microlessons and animated videos for the internal content control system. Articulate Rise supports rapid development of structured e-learning modules, while CreateStudio 3 brings visual engagement through animations. Together, these tools improve content quality while significantly reducing production costs when compared to outsourcing or developing custom platforms from scratch.

2. AI Research Application

Artificial Intelligence is reshaping the education technology sector, and several applications could further enhance operations at the employer.

OpenAI’s ChatGPT API

This conversational AI could be integrated into the survey platform to provide students and staff with 24/7 support on survey participation, SEL guidance, and system navigation. It can also help generate auto-responses for common administrative inquiries.

Descript’s Overdub and AI Editing Tools

For content production, Descript’s AI voice tools can support faster narration revisions without requiring re-recordings, and the AI-editing timeline can speed up video refinement. This reduces turnaround time and increases content agility.

Natural Language Processing for Survey Insights

Advanced NLP tools, such as MonkeyLearn or IBM Watson, can enhance survey analysis by categorizing open-ended responses, identifying sentiment trends, and flagging concerns in real time. This would equip the survey platform with deeper data insights and quicker interventions.

3. Advice Received

The best advice I received this week came from my supervisor during our content sprint review: “Progress beats perfection. Ship, then refine.” This advice helped me stay focused on iteration rather than stalling for polish. I applied it by pushing through initial drafts of microlesson visuals for UE04 and scheduling time for quality improvements post-review, rather than waiting for an elusive “perfect” version.

The longer-running version of that advice has been the ETTO principle I picked up earlier in the ITAM program. I tend toward thoroughness by default and have to be deliberate about ensuring time efficiencies to balance. Reframing the supervisor’s note as the same trade-off, applied at the scale of a single microlesson draft rather than a quarter of work, made it easier to act on in the moment.

4. Discussion Board Reflection

While working on UE06: Controlled Breathing, I learned how deceptively simple animations can carry powerful emotional weight, especially for younger audiences. The focus of this lesson was a calm breathing exercise guided by a gently animated sun. My initial challenge was identifying the right platform to execute this vision effectively. I prototyped in both Adobe Premiere and Canva, but ultimately found those tools too limiting for the kind of looping animation and timing precision I needed.

After some experimentation, I chose to rebuild the animation directly in CreateStudio, using layered image components of the sun and sky. Constructing the animation within the platform enabled me to control the pacing of inhalation and exhalation more effectively, aligning the visual cues with the calming intent of the exercise.

This project reminded me of the iterative nature of the creative process and how the tools you choose must align with the emotional tone you are trying to convey.
