About this paper

A loyalty program proposal authored from inside an EdTech SaaS company. The piece designs a multi-modal program (subscription, tier-based, mission-based, free perks, community), defines three customer segments with distinct relationships to revenue and mission, and specifies the KPIs needed to actually run it — CAC, CLV, expected CLV, CLV/CAC ratio, NPS, churn, engagement, loyalty, ARPU/ARPA, ARR/MRR. The closing section moves from metric to motion: how a values-driven organization translates data into community engagement and meaningful rewards without losing its forever promise.

Why it matters to the practice

Loyalty programs in B2B SaaS are not punch cards. The work is in segmenting who actually buys, who uses, and who is served, then choosing the metrics that tell the truth about each relationship. That triple lens — Ambassador (decision maker), Administrator (daily user), Respondent (mission target) — has stayed with me. It maps cleanly onto procurement and engagement work I do in the studio, and it shows up again in product design where 'the user' is rarely one person. The KPI stack here is the operational expression of customer love.

Connections

Pairs directly with the RMT 340 buyer's-experience paper: that one analyzed the salesperson, this one designs the program. Both treat customer relationships as systems that can be designed, measured, and improved rather than performances of friendliness. Sits next to the ADMG track on operations and the IT track on data-driven decision making. Identifying details have been generalized; the structure and analysis are unchanged.

TO: Jose Riera FROM: Joshua Keyes DATE: December 3, 2023 SUBJECT: Loyalty Program Project, Final

Loyalty Program Summary

About the Organization

The organization is an EdTech SaaS company specializing in web-based applications designed for educational institutions and school districts. The flagship product is a comprehensive survey platform that administers and reports on surveys conducted among students, families, and school staff. The platform fosters wellness and wholeness, promoting Social-Emotional Learning (SEL) and enhancing school climate.

Established in 2018 and based in Southern California, the organization is dedicated to serving the Education Technology industry. It strives to be the servant leaders people turn to for remarkable solutions to create a kinder world. Its mission is to create tools that people love to use to achieve success. Solutions are born from a mindful blend of passion and action. Through expert guidance, humble collaboration, and the implementation of contemporary technology, the team works to create transformative solutions that foster happiness and well-being.

Motivations of Study

I am studying this company because I am employed by the organization, and as a stakeholder I am passionate about the mission, vision, and values it currently holds. Student wellness, excellent engagement rates, and higher quality interactions between teachers and students that reveal their benefits through captured data and statistical representation allow governing agencies to make more informed decisions and bring about impactful positive change. I am attracted to the intrinsic rewards offered by conducting our business well.

Additionally, in choosing a place of employment I do so with regard to challenges that may allow continuous growth. My title of Project Manager is direct, but the roles I carry out are multifaceted and demanding, and present an opportunity for bettering the organization’s competency, compliance, and culture. I hope to apply creativity to initiate productive processes that align with the values of the organization and myself.

Loyalty Program Initiative

The organization does not currently have a loyalty program in place. I am proposing developing a loyalty program for current and future system administrators. The program is a combination of several program types:

  • Subscription. A digital membership area to connect with for questions, ideas, and testimonials related to the survey platform.
  • Tier-based. There are currently two tiers of the product for customers to choose from: a basic tier that includes support for student and staff surveys, and a second tier that adds family surveys, where customers may gather feedback from parents.
  • Mission-based. The mission is clear: to increase student wellness by increasing the quality of school culture and climate through the use of surveys and support built on a foundation of social-emotional learning (SEL) practices. The product makes safer and healthier schools that listen and act on the needs of students.
  • Free perks. Promotional gifts that include the organization’s branding and that are centered on student wellness and related to teaching, student, and SEL culture.
  • Community. Invitations to discuss future product features and improvements. Member highlights through interviews published on the organization’s social channels and website.

Client Collaboration: Performance Metrics

The organization will monitor contemporary loyalty program status and make loyalty tracking enhancements through collaboration with clients, gaining success through data as key performance indicators.

Current Loyalty Program Status

The organization utilizes a Customer Service Management (CSM) platform for sales and account work. Sales and customer service managers use the system to track prospects and record notes pertaining to clients and possible account acquisitions. In conjunction, the organization utilizes a customer support management platform. This system allows the team to receive and act on customer needs quickly. It also includes a vital self-service component that the team uses to compile a wide range of support and help documentation consumed by clients and the team itself.

Though the organization has tools to track the needs of customers and support structures to react quickly and provide quality service, it does not currently have any overt metrics that are being tracked on a consistent basis.

Loyalty Tracking Enhancements

The organization should implement consistent tracking metrics to increase understanding of target customers and the resulting revenue from actions and adjustments of its product and service offerings. There are three groups the company must be keenly aware of tracking, as their interaction with the company is very different.

  • Ambassador. Those who are the decision makers or primary advisors on whether or not the product will be purchased.
  • Administrators. Those who are the daily users, and most likely the Promoters, Passives, or Detractors of the product. They heavily influence the Ambassador group.
  • Respondents. Those who are receiving and taking surveys and are the primary target of the mission to increase wellness. These break into three categories of users: school students, school staff, and student family.

Active Holistic Client Understanding

By analyzing and monitoring appropriate KPIs, the organization can respond quickly to external market changes and internal quality considerations. Leveraging data will help the organization understand customer value, customer behavior, and how to appropriately enhance client and company success and viability.

Customer Value. The organization must understand and be diligent about how customers interact with revenue to clarify how we are arriving at and impacting the bottom line. To understand these factors, the organization should track and manage the following key performance indicators.

  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC). How much the organization spends to acquire a new customer, calculated by dividing marketing and sales costs by new customers during a given period. Assists in determining the effectiveness of marketing and sales budgets.
  • Customer Lifetime Value (CLV). From acquisition to subscription cancellation between a given time, this metric determines how much revenue is directly or indirectly derived on average from a customer. Calculated by dividing the revenue earned by the months subscribed.
  • Expected CLV. Helps determine behaviors within cohorts. Calculated by dividing ARPU by Churn Rate.
  • CLV/CAC Ratio. Allows the company to determine the return on investment of current and future expenditures on acquisition costs.

Customer Behavior. The organization should position itself to be keenly aware of customer behavior, as it will indicate changes in product-market climate over time. Understanding these changes allows the organization to manage change effectively for continued success and ensure high-quality product solutions that fit customers’ needs.

  • Net Promoter Score (NPS). A scaled 1 to 10 questionnaire directed at customer satisfaction with product and service offerings. Scores of 9 to 10 are considered Promoters, 7 to 8 are Passives, and 6 and below are Detractors of the organization’s offerings. This allows for an understanding of performance on given aspects of the product and service provided.
  • Churn. Determined by subscribers or revenue canceling within a given period divided by the subscribers or revenue at the start of the period. This indicates how many customers the company is losing relative to total customers. It is a more critical metric than CAC to monitor, as it influences CLV and product fit for target customers in practice.
  • Customer Engagement. Through careful tracking of application features by the user, we can determine the breadth and depth of utilization by clients of the product. This can be useful in understanding friction points and the aspects of the application that are used by healthy customers as well as those who may cancel or cease application utilization.
  • Loyalty. In addition to using NPS to track customer value, we will apply a metric to customer behavior as a way to understand satisfaction in conjunction with monthly visits to the platform dashboard. This ratio will help us qualify NPS scores by user engagement. Additionally, surveys conducted by Administrators should be tracked and associated with this metric as a way to gain insight into the frequency of completed product deliverables the customer is receiving. Decisions can be made to determine the value of interaction broadly in comparison to data deliverables acquisition. This can be further informed by accessing customer favorability scores over time on configurations that have been administered consistently.

Company Success and Viability. While the organization comprises servant leaders, it is essential to determine the viability of continued service by being attentive to revenue generation and fluctuations of product market changes. This ensures continued responsible resource attribution and management.

  • Average Revenue per User (ARPU) or Account (ARPA). Calculated by taking total monthly recurring revenue divided by total users or accounts. These metrics can gain insight into customer cohort trends. When aggregated across the three customer groups (Ambassador, Administrator, Respondent), it gives insight into the value the company is offering and the revenue it is receiving from the multiple groups. These indicators help determine future pricing for healthy growth and indicate early weakness in sales, service, or product aspects of business operations.
  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) and Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR). Recurring revenue metric arrived at by dividing monthly or yearly revenue by total users of the given period. This metric will give the organization indicators of company value. Often this is the primary metric to indicate company valuation and is necessary to determine success trends and financial dealings with potential partners and vendors.

Adjusting practices through revelations derived by carefully considering collected data will help the organization achieve more excellent customer value, react to customer behaviors, and ensure company success and viability.

EdTech Enrichment: Maintaining Engagement

Enrichment of the EdTech space is a priority of the organization. We conduct all our business with a servant-leader philosophy. This means that maintaining engagement in any loyalty program should prioritize value-based strategies that deliver meaningful customer offerings.

Values-Based Strategies

The organization will maintain loyal customer engagement through continuous monitoring of KPIs and measured, informed changes to customer service programs that coincide with the organization’s forever promise. These efforts are directed toward the ideal customer persona of each of the outlined customer groups: Ambassador, Administrator, and Respondent.

Community Engagement

Products are developed with the purpose of bettering our world by engaging the EdTech space meaningfully, in order to improve student wellness and deliver the best opportunities possible for our future generation. For this reason, the organization is motivated to invest in initiatives that present best practices and inform philosophies that will continue to champion student success, as well as the organizations within the community that honor similar commitments. The organization will dedicate itself to deliberately and effectively interact and cultivate our shared mission and vision through collaboration with community activists and organizations.

  • Engage SEL and EdTech community leaders. Manage contact relationships through email, SMS, conferences, and community events.
  • Engage SEL and EdTech organizations. Manage organization relationships through email, SMS, conferences, and community events.
  • Invest in, promote, and engage authors. Social media and relationship management and engagement through email and meetings in order to build relationships and perspectives.
  • Procure specialists and researchers. Actively seek and manage information and contacts to further community understanding through webinars, articles, and conferences.

Meaningful Offerings

In addressing the Subscriber and Tier-Based elements of our loyalty program, we will offer standardized benefits that continuously meet customers’ use cases while remaining dynamic in addressing revealed deficiencies through data analysis. This ensures an excellent customer-to-product fit. These are the consistent cornerstone of our forever promise:

  • Expectation Setting. Setting expectations early and often while communicating results regularly.
  • Relationship Management. Managing relationship roadmaps while mapping consistent customer experiences.
  • Service First. Easy and effective customer assistance with a focus on empathy, convenience, and timely service.
  • Friction Free. Removal of as much friction and as many pain points as possible, with a focus on continuously exceeding customer expectations.
  • Customer Management Platforms. Continued investment into customer service tools, including the sales CRM, the support platform, and increasing customer service managers and developers as necessary to ensure record keeping, communications, and resolution of issues.

Mission-based and Community elements of the program will be more dynamic and unique to the customer group and situation. Currently, CSMs meet with customers that include those experienced in our platform and the positive outcomes that have resulted. We have identified some of these gracious professionals and have highlighted them through social media via testimonial clips. We should consider presenting a more thorough account by capturing the full story of the situation, individuals, and outcomes. This will allow the organization to dynamically and meaningfully engage in the community that prioritizes student wellness through the healthiest school culture and climate possible.

  • Community Impact. Making memories around shared success that is representative of our mission, through community enrichment. SEL improvement success posts via the organization’s website and social channels, as well as video highlights of student wellness and SEL community effects, will be effective for marketing and for furthering SEL knowledge. This could be presented as an article in a new section of the website along with a three- to five-minute video that celebrates the success while emphasizing student wellness impact, the individual who took initiative, and how the organization’s support and services assisted in results.
  • Review, Rework, Reward. Asking for feedback and acting on the gathered information while offering relevant free perks to inform and enable mission-based SEL knowledge and understanding.
  • Reciprocity. Surprise and trumpeted reciprocity through gifts of swag geared toward awareness of SEL culture and the organization’s role within the community.

Free perks will be uniquely relevant to the organization and its mission of supporting student wellness and SEL concepts. Gifts, swag, and inclusivity programs will improve, sustain, and share student wellness resources and messaging. These rewards will be tuned to regions and groups to ensure they are meaningful to local education institutions, while maintaining sensitivity to individual organizations’ mandates and missions. Examples are not limited to but could include the following.

  • Learning materials. Books, programs, and online courses.
  • Awareness swag. T-shirts, mugs, keychains, stickers, and office stationery with brand and SEL-relevant messaging.
  • Product improvement loops. Feedback, roadmap, and product-feedback meetings with invested customers and product developers periodically.
  • Community experts. Webinars with noted authors, researchers, and experts in the fields of student wellness and SEL.
  • Partner appreciation. Special discounts or free use of partner applications and services that also further and make a positive impact in the EdTech space.
  • Customer appreciation. Recognition through the organization’s community channels, providing free marketing opportunities for educational institutions that are leading successful student wellness initiatives.

Conclusion

Implementation of a dedicated loyalty program that includes client collaboration through performance metrics and EdTech enrichment through maintained engagement will inform the organization’s customer forever promise: a brighter future through student wellness success, tomorrow.
