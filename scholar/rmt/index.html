Retail Management Technology

RMT coursework looks at retail and customer-facing work from the operational side. Influence, relationship management, and career planning sit inside the same broader question I keep arriving at from the design and product side: how do you build something the person on the other end actually finds usable, trustworthy, and worth coming back to.

  1. RMT 366 Final Project Dec 3, 2023

    Loyalty Program Project

    A loyalty program proposal authored from inside an EdTech SaaS company. The piece designs a multi-modal program (subscription, tier-based, mission-based, free perks, community), defines three customer segments with distinct relationships to revenue and mission, and specifies the KPIs needed to actually run it — CAC, CLV, expected CLV, CLV/CAC ratio, NPS, churn, engagement, loyalty, ARPU/ARPA, ARR/MRR. The closing section moves from metric to motion: how a values-driven organization translates data into community engagement and meaningful rewards without losing its forever promise.

  2. RMT 340 Final Project Jun 2, 2023

    The Buyer's Experience

    A long-form analysis of a real B2B procurement engagement from the buyer's side: a SOC 2 auditing firm being evaluated for selection. The frameworks of the course (Trust-Based Selling, ADAPT, SPIN, BATCH, SIER, LAARC) are applied to the conversation as it actually happened, not as a textbook exercise.

  3. RMT 379 M04 Feb 26, 2023

    Three Facts, Three Questions

    A short reflective exercise framed as informational due diligence on a prospective internship sponsor. Three facts about the organization (mission, conceptual framework, platform dependency) and three open questions about its trajectory (mission ceiling, sustainability under platform shifts, scope of who counts as a student). The format is brief but the questions are the real work: they preview what an employee should actually be curious about before joining.
