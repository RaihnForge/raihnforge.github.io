About this paper

The opening framing assignment for the RMT 366 course project. Two or three sentences naming the company and product whose customer loyalty program would be explored across the rest of the quarter, plus the value proposition that makes the program worth designing in the first place.

Why it matters to the practice

Most worthwhile work starts as a short paragraph that names the subject without overexplaining it. This framing names an EdTech SaaS company, the survey platform it operates, the population it serves, and what the customer actually receives. Each later artifact in the course built on top of this paragraph, which is why the discipline of writing a tight first frame matters.

Connections

Pairs with the RMT 366 loyalty program final, which is the full design treatment built on top of this framing. Together they trace the arc from project pitch to operational proposal. Identifying details have been generalized; the analytical content is unchanged.

A note on naming. The original paper named the specific EdTech employer and its flagship product. For public posting, both have been generalized: “the EdTech employer” and “the survey platform.” The analytical content is preserved. The intent was always portfolio reflection on the discipline of project framing, not commentary on any specific commercial entity.

TO: Course Instructor FROM: Joshua Keyes DATE: September 30, 2023 SUBJECT: Loyalty Program Description, A01

Project Subject

Please write two or three sentences describing the company, organization, or business concept whose customer loyalty program you wish to explore for your course project.

The EdTech employer is a software development company with a focus on student-centered learning. Their primary product is a survey platform that performs informational survey and data-gathering work, enabling an analytical understanding of class, school, and district conditions for the purpose of increasing student wellness and education effectiveness. Clients receive access to the platform, data management and storage, and customer support.
