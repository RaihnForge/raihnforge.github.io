**SUBJECT:** Three Facts, Three Questions, M04

## Organization Information

The following are three facts and three questions regarding the organization I will likely be interning with during my pursuit of an information technology administration management degree in project management, and hopefully cybersecurity. The organization is an EdTech SaaS company whose flagship product is a survey platform for student wellness data in K through 12 schools.

## Facts

### 1. Objective and Mission

The organization's mission and operating objectives center on making students and teachers more capable and humanizing the classroom. These goals are pursued through a survey platform built for students, staff, and families in the K through 12 education space. The platform primarily collects student wellness information and provides actionable statistics to help educators understand what is going well and what needs more attention in the classroom. The product offers tools and services to advise on next steps after observation, namely identifying and improving areas of issue. In this way the organization achieves its mission by equipping educators to enable students to be the best they can be.

### 2. SEL: Social-Emotional Learning

Social-Emotional Learning plays a large role in the platform. Though SEL as a construct is in no way mechanically linked to the application itself, or to its architectural utilization, it is a marked framework that educators, namely primary clients in California, use as a way to impact student wellness, a subject that is at the core of the product. Many of the organization's webinars pertain to SEL understanding, and several of its employees are SEL subject experts.

### 3. Platform Dependency

The web application is largely dependent on a major cloud productivity suite and the corresponding browser and device ecosystem that have been broadly adopted in the K through 12 education space. There are strengths in focusing on this market segment that allow emphasis, efficiency, and effective production.

## Questions

### 1. What level of the mission is considered achieved, and what is next?

Given the organization's mission and the premiere product, what plans does the company have to continue adding value and growing the effectiveness of mission success? I see that identifying the problem is the first step to getting better, and SEL offers a way to understand what steps may improve problem areas. I wonder how leadership sees software, human services, or the framework itself growing in the future.

### 2. Given the mission, what are the dependency and sustainability plans?

A strength of the organization seems to be its focus on a specific cloud productivity suite and device ecosystem in combination with the K through 12 education space. While these are good pillars to build a product from, the sustainability of a technology-based organization comes from its ability to change and adapt to shifts in market needs and new technologies. Will the product family widen its services to students and teachers who might not be on those platforms?

### 3. Does the mission of empowering students include higher education?

The platform is an incredible tool, and improving student lives is important. I am curious about the considerations to utilize the wellness data-gathering tool in expanded situations. More and more college courses, particularly during the COVID epidemic, have moved online. It is now much less likely that professors and fellow students can observe and report on the wellness of their peers. Would it be appropriate for the organization to also move into a wider market in the higher education space?
