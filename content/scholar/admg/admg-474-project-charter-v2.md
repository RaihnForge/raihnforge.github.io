## Executive Summary

Central Medical Center is a facility with the highest expectation of quality and concern for our community and patients. Recently it has become apparent that our technology for staff and clients is running the risk of failing to meet our organizational vision, values, and mission. For this reason, the initiative to refresh all technology in the facility has been enacted. This first phase of the technology refresh will set the tone for the following stages and bring the organization in line ethically while curtailing future time and cost expenditures.

The project, costing $500,000 over six months, will conduct a technological refresh by updating operating systems on all 101 nurse computer (laptop and desktop) workstations on floors 3 to 5. The systems will be updated to Windows 10 and brought into compliance with application ecosystem, network conduct, and cyber security standards. The four-stage Phase 1 project will bring the organization in line with internal and external industry standards and expectations while saving on costly maintenance and potential failure expenditures.

## Project Purpose and Justification

A technology refresh is necessary to maintain high quality standards, reduce future maintenance costs, increase security, and increase efficiency for our stakeholders. Phase 1 will initiate the facility-wide refresh, prioritizing increased quality in the areas that represent our first-line interaction with clients.

### Business Need and Case

Professional consultants have evaluated our current workstations and technological ecosystem and have determined that we will soon reach a critical point of increased risk to the total effectiveness and financial competency of our current setup. Without Phase 1, we will be increasingly ineffective in several core areas of service deployment. Costs will rise from additional maintenance, lack of security, desync with industry-standard services, and lost potential energy expenditure benefits. Time efficiency will be lacking due to slower systems, increased failure rates, and neglected performance increase opportunity via more capable digital workstations. Scope management will be ill aligned, because we will not be able to offer best quality and will become preoccupied with increasing projects addressing system failures.

### Business Objectives

Several key objectives this opportunity supports serve the organizational strategic plan. The financial advances in executing Phase 1 reduce organizational expenditures in maintenance and increase effectiveness of current staffing. Without Phase 1, additional personnel and salary allocation would be needed to address security, software, and hardware concerns. Removing these potential expenditures keeps the organization in step with its lean and focused employment directives. Our vision, mission, and values unify under the importance of quality, as is appropriate for a health care service organization. Refusing to maintain or exceed industry standard would constitute a denial of our core ethical organizational objectives, and that cannot be seen as a valid path forward.

## Project Description

The Nurse Workstation Refresh Phase 1 project will bring the facility's workstations and technology into compliance with organizational core values for floors 3 to 5 of Central Medical Center. The project will be completed in stages led by Joshua Keyes (Project Manager), directed by Dr. Phylicia Patterson (Project Sponsor), and supported by the Nursing Coordinator, the IT Coordinator, the Master Load Team, the Install Team, IT Assists, and the Clean-up Team.

Development and testing of the cloneable Master Load and ecosystem integration simulations form Stage A. In Stage B, each floor is updated individually, with Medical Center daily operations redirected to active floors during the work. After each floor's refresh, the digital ecosystem for that floor is evaluated and approved. The process is then validated for compliance and reliability in Stage C with thorough stress testing by IT and Cyber Security. Finally, Stage D executes feedback derived from staff concerns gathered throughout the prior stages. The conclusion of Phase 1 will take no longer than six months and will stay within the $500,000 budget.

### Requirements

For the project to be considered a success, the following must be executed:

- Success on all named deliverables (see Project Deliverables)
- Completion of Phase 1 nurse workstation refresh on floors 3 to 5
- Stakeholders (patients, owners, staff, sponsor) reasonably satisfied
- Scope adhered to, with quality and stakeholder satisfaction maintained
- Constraints respected: under the $500,000 budget, within the 6-month timeline, within agreed scope (scope creep avoided)
- Communication is constant: within teams, among teams, with staff, with clients, with sponsor, and reasonable updates to other stakeholders

### Constraints

The project must stay within its allocated budget of $500,000. All deliverables must conclude by the end of the dedicated Phase 1 timeline of 6 months. The project will utilize currently employed teams when and wherever possible, to mitigate communication, quality, and security risks. Quality of the concluded project will be monitored and controlled by the Project Manager and Project Sponsor in keeping with stakeholder input and advice. Equipment will be acquired through currently associated vendors, with costs approved by Project Manager and Project Sponsor.

The project must not take on responsibilities of other departments and must not subvert Phase 1 priorities to other perceived phase priorities or constraints.

### Assumptions

The project team assumes the following:

- Priority use of time for the Master Load Team, Install Team, IT Assists, and Clean-up Team located in Central Medical Center
- Network infrastructure has been preinstalled and validated
- Computer hardware concerns have been addressed by other projects
- Workstation logistics and ergonomics have been addressed and updated through prior project allocation
- Majority of software licenses have been previously purchased and accounted for
- Reduced appointment capacity considerations have been made
- Training and certification of OS is accounted for in other projects

### Preliminary Scope Statement

Phase 1 of the Nurse Workstation Refresh Initiative is the complete updating of the operating system on all 101 systems currently servicing floors 3 to 5 of Central Medical Center to Windows 10. The refresh includes validation testing for assumed application ecosystem, established network communication, and cyber security standards. The work will require no more than $500,000 and will complete in no more than 6 months from initiation. Equipment will be focused on currently available on-site utilities. Staffing includes the Project Manager, Project Sponsor, Nursing Coordinator, IT Coordinator, Master Load Team, Install Team, IT Assists, and Clean-up Team. All directives will consider the input of stakeholders and the evaluation of the Project Sponsor.

## Risks

Risk assessment and quality control are paramount because of the technical complexities involved and the potential mortal implications to clients if the project is not navigated successfully. The fully loaded software and configuration master to be cloned to each machine must be rigorously assessed and tested before any rollout begins.

| Risk | Result |
| :--- | :--- |
| Master Clone Efficacy | An infected, ineffective, or corrupted master boot would force a full re-refresh of all systems. |
| System Failure | Loss of time, client casualties, and loss of organizational and public confidence could result from unstable systems. |
| Incompatible Software | Migration of applications to the new OS could surface issues that IT teams have to contend with. Vendors may need to be called to restore functionality. |
| Scope Creep | Being Phase 1 of a larger initiative increases the likelihood of bleed between project and initiative processes and phases. |
| Communication | The Medical Center remains operational during the project; careful and accurate communication is key to reducing friction and bottlenecks. |
| Time Allocation | Carefully coordinated efforts and processes are required to reduce slack time and maintain efficient completion of deliverables. |

The impact and severity of potential failure on a project this sophisticated could be catastrophic from a human perspective as well as organizationally. Detailing responses to almost-certain hang-ups is crucial. There is little room for quality variance, which reduces maneuverability across the remaining constraints.

## Project Approval Requirements

The project will be considered successfully complete when all objectives (see Project Description, Project Objectives and Success Criteria) are deemed met by the Project Sponsor, the Director of Nursing. Successful completion will be measured by the requirements (see Project Description, Requirements) as reviewed by the Project Sponsor.

Project exit criteria include going over budget by more than 30 percent, missing any milestone by more than 50 percent of its allocated time, or revealed or perceived maliciousness or incompetence of management as deemed by the Project Sponsor.

## Project Manager

The Project Manager for Nurse Workstation Refresh Phase 1 is Joshua Keyes. Keyes will be responsible for managing, leading, and executing on all agreed-upon objectives and deliverables. Keyes will have high-level authority as overseen and approved by the Project Sponsor. Keyes will maintain constant communication with the Project Sponsor and provide reasonable updates to stakeholders upon request. Budgetary spending authority is granted to Keyes but is subject to weekly and intermittent review by the Project Sponsor. Scheduling may be directly or indirectly influenced by Keyes.
