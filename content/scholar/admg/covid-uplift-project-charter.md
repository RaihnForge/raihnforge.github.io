## Business Case

### Introduction and Background

COVID Relief's core objective is to provide information, resources, and knowledge to those affected by COVID-19 in Kittitas County. By providing an all-in-one resource of information, the project streamlines time and energy for residents and connects the community.

### Business Objective

COVID Relief's goal is to connect residents who are struggling with the pandemic to a place for resources and community. The project improves accessibility by providing a website that has an array of information.

### Current Situation and Problem/Opportunity Statement

There are thousands of resources available to those affected by COVID-19, but it can be hard to sift through all of the data. Our site combines multiple resources into one location, making it easier for residents to locate virtual information and giving the community a place to connect and share support. The project will also make connections with additional stakeholders to invest in the work.

### Critical Assumption and Constraints

The website must be of value to those using it. We provide resources that are within the community we are serving, and we are varied enough in information to cover multiple demographics and ensure inclusion.

### Analysis of Options and Recommendation

There are four options for this project:

1. Through initial investigation, the time and resource investment would not achieve the project goal, so we opt not to do the project.
2. Invest and create a static website designed by volunteers as a one-time upload. The site would not be maintained or updated. Connect with community partners to create additional funding.
3. Volunteers find the content and hire web consultants to build the site, which is maintained and updated with current information on a monthly basis. Connect with community partners to create additional funding.
4. Outsource to a consulting firm to do web design and implement an interactive website that is updated and maintained. Connect with community partners to create additional funding.

Based on discussions with stakeholders, we believe option 4 is best.

### Preliminary Project Requirements

The main features of the website include:

1. Unemployment links and assistance
2. Food bank and food stamp assistance
3. Child education and support
4. Financial assistance
5. Wellness, mental health, and humor
6. A community blackboard for connection

### Budget Estimate and Financial Analysis

A preliminary estimate of cost for this entire project is $100,000. This includes the cost of hiring a company to build a website and pay a staff of five to gather information and create the site content.

### Schedule Estimate

The sponsor would like to see the project completed in three months, by March 18.

### Potential Risks

This project carries low risk because it is a nonprofit. The foremost risk is ensuring information is up to date and accurate. It is also important to make sure all links work and phone numbers are accurate and working. Information needs to be a link rather than the source, and liability disclaimers need to be in place.

## Team Contract

S.M.A.R.T. goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-Bound.

**Project Name:** COVID Uplift and Informational Website.
**Sign-off date:** January 25, 2021. All four teammates signed (Joshua Keyes, Teammate 1, Teammate 2, Teammate 3).

### Code of Conduct

As a project team, we will:

- Divide the workload evenly among teammates and distribute tasks based on each member's skills and role.
- Participate in team meetings on time and communicate effectively.
- Avoid deadlines that are being set too aggressively, to reduce future delays.
- Contribute our best effort to every single task given.
- Coordinate efficiently through Teams and a text message group. Convey ideas and voice out constructive opinions at any time.
- Set achievable deadlines and help each other whenever a problem is faced.

### Participation

We will:

- Be present at all meetings and be prepared in advance.
- Bring complete research materials and information as needed.
- Share ideas and voice opinions accordingly.
- Address relevant topics each week and discuss them one at a time.
- Summarize team meeting notes to save important points.
- Embrace others' opinions and try to integrate those that are constructive. Use a voting system if needed.

### Communication

We will:

- Use Microsoft Teams for virtual meetings.
- Use text message groups for simple scheduling and exchanging ideas.
- Present ideas and data in a clear manner.
- Listen attentively to others when they are presenting ideas.

### Problem Solving

We will:

- Try to think outside the box and be innovative while solving problems.
- Use research data and materials to back up a statement.
- Discuss problems faced as soon as possible to address them quickly.
- Brainstorm on different aspects and research online.
- Resolve conflicts among teammates and find a solution together.

### Meeting Guidelines

We will:

- Hold meetings every Monday at 5pm, facilitated by Zoom.
- Discuss the impact of the discussion and evaluate it accordingly.
- Try to accommodate every teammate's time and find the best meeting day of the week.
- Ensure enough meeting time for discussion and summarizing outcomes.
