## What Is an Informational Interview

Informational interviews are a tool that can help one increase understanding of career possibilities, gauge desire to pursue them, and raise awareness of possible employment (FiskeJul, 2008). They allow the interviewee to learn more about the industry, the organization, and possible employment opportunities that may be of interest (Knight, 2016). The primary objective is to deeply understand the realities of a particular occupation (Knight, 2016). That understanding allows one to orient self-assessment tools such as SWOT, SMART goals, and personal development planning in a focused way that is more directly aimed at achieving a specific career goal.

Job interviews and informational interviews are similar in that an interested party meets with a representative of an employer to discuss career territory. Their objectives are very different. Job interviews are focused on determining whether a person's current skills and knowledge are viable for a specific open position. An informational interview is more open. It allows an individual to ask questions of someone who is currently working in a field or industry of interest (Doyle, 2019). The goal should be to derive specific information from the professional, and much less to advocate current qualifications (Bomzer, 2003). In a job interview, the applicant should be talking roughly 80 percent of the time and the prospective employer will be directing the conversation. In an informational interview, the professional should be speaking roughly 80 percent of the time and the interested party should be leading the discussion (Bomzer, 2003).

## Benefits

An informational interview provides an opportunity to discuss organizational needs in a setting where stress levels are lower and the parties can meet as equals to gain insider information and begin a relationship (Doyle, 2019). Carrying out interviews increases soft skills, awareness, and access to organizations (Plakhotnik, 2017). One may even discover an inside track on positions opening up that are not yet known to the public (Bomzer, 2003). If a job interview later occurs, the applicant is likely to have greater self-confidence and conduct themselves more professionally (Doyle, 2019). Prospects of obtaining employment may also increase through the demonstrated interest in the organization and position (Bomzer, 2003). The process opens further networking opportunities within the organization and the broader field (Knight, 2016). Importantly, initiative and independence from third-party placement programs are gained, empowering the applicant to confidently chart their own career path (Plakhotnik, 2017).

## Steps

The literature offers many step lists for conducting an informational interview. Knight (2016) emphasizes preparing and practicing, setting the tone, and thinking like a journalist. Zhang (2014) focuses on warming up, gathering the required information, and networking. Bomzer (2003) frames it as planning, background research, conducting the interview, and analyzing results to refine the process. The following is a synthesis drawn from these sources.

1. **Find a subject.** Use personal networking, professional social networks such as LinkedIn, and targeted internet searches to identify organizations and people you are interested in (Fiske, 2008).
2. **Study the organization.** Understand the organization first so questions are not asked that could be answered with a basic public search (Doyle, 2019). Researching the organization also tells the applicant whether the employer is one they actually want to work for, supports a more informed application and cover letter, and improves later job-interview performance (Indeed, 2021).
3. **Prepare questions.** Carefully craft questions that consider what information matters at the applicant's current stage in the job-search process (Zhang, 2014).
4. **Conduct the interview.** Respect the interviewee's time by being efficient and bounded. Take notes during the conversation, and summarize the meeting at the end to confirm understanding (Doyle, 2019).
5. **Write a thank-you letter.** Keep it brief. A handwritten note is more memorable than email and should be sent as soon as possible (Knight, 2016).
6. **Analyze.** Consider the outcomes and refine the questions for future interviews (Bomzer, 2003). Feed gained information into self-assessment tools, and consider coaching and mentorship options.

## Organization and Process

### Summary of the Organization

For this assignment I researched a major independent game development studio based in Bellevue, Washington. Over more than thirty years the studio has shipped multiple successful game franchises and is in an active expansion phase, with focus on continuing development of its flagship live-service franchise while broadening into additional platforms, franchises, and international markets (personal communication, June 7, 2021).

The studio develops products across web, mobile, PC, and current and prior-generation consoles. Its intellectual properties extend into licensed comic books, novels, apparel, board games, toys, and other collectibles. It is in a phase of growth, with stated ambitions toward international audiences and a diversified platform footprint.

A studio of this scale requires a wide range of disciplines to execute its go-to-market strategies. These include art, audio, business operations, consumer products, engineering, game design, information technology, narrative, production, project management, publishing and marketing, testing and QA, and UI and UX design.

The organization is led by a CEO and a CCO, with an estimated headcount in the high hundreds. Publicly stated mission, values, and vision statements emphasize creating hopeful worlds that inspire player communities, prioritizing player experience and team strength over heroics, holding strong ideas loosely, closing as an everyday practice, widening perspective, keeping it fun, and aspiring to become a multi-franchise entertainment company.

Independence is an important element of the organization's stated culture. The studio also leans into supporting and recruiting for diversity, including an internal inclusion club and visible support for LGBTQ communities. International recruiting is part of how the studio sources talent (personal communication, June 7, 2021).

Charitable activity is part of the studio's public profile. The studio's foundation describes its mission as reducing distress and suffering in children through entertainment, partnering with humanitarian aid organizations in times of natural disaster and crisis. A signature initiative provides iPads to children in challenging medical and life circumstances for therapy and distraction.

The studio is notable in the industry for having previously been acquired by a major platform holder and later regaining its independence, an outcome that is rare for a successful studio of its size. The studio also dissolved a multi-year publishing partnership in 2019 and is now self-publishing. A new international office is under development, presumably to support the broader publishing strategy, and the studio continues to ship expansions for its flagship franchise alongside unannounced projects.

### The Interview

I conducted an informational interview with a senior engineer at the studio on June 7, 2021. The interviewee holds an Engineering Architect title with a lead role on a graphics-incubation team. He is a longtime network contact and friend. I chose this contact because we have overlapping interests in the same industry, similar values, and a shared long-running conversation about the positive impacts of game development and its multifaceted disciplines. We had both held similar dream careers in the field from a very young age. He has worked at the studio for over a decade and in the broader game-development industry for longer.

Information and understanding of the inner workings, technologies, and management styles of the studio are intimately understood by a lead-level graphics engineer. The interviewee has conducted job interviews as part of the studio's hiring process. He is familiar with project management styles, discipline processes, leadership communication, and conflict styles and resolution. He uses and understands the technical, soft, and conceptual skills required to be successful at the studio.

The interview considered questions in five categories developed from research and from the ADMG 371 course. Organizational research was conducted in advance to understand the studio's history, structure, market position, and future direction. Mission, values, and vision statements, along with organizational culture and structure, were particularly important to understand prior to the conversation. The question set was organized around VanHuss and Kreitner's (2014) "Required for Success" skills framework, drawn from the textbook's discussion of organizational overview and the three skill domains (technical, soft, conceptual), and informed by suggestions from Zhang (2014) and Doyle (2019).

### Introduction Used at the Start of the Interview

Good evening. My name is Joshua Keyes. I am conducting an informational interview for professional career growth and to facilitate a college course assignment. My interests, skills, and experience include project management, game development, freelance project management, graphics, and leadership and supervision. I would like to inquire about five specific areas regarding your professional experience: organizational structure, personal experiences and insights, industry thoughts, desired skills (education and experience), and practical and functional considerations. I am grateful for your time and hope to take no longer than thirty minutes. Please feel free to pass on any questions you may not feel comfortable answering. I realize there may be intellectual property, technical, or procedural information that is not appropriate to share. If there are any questions that could be more easily answered through publicly available sources, please point me there so I take as little of your time as possible.

## Interview Questions

### Warm-Up and Organizational Structure

- How did you get your start in this field?
- What is your title and how does that role fit within the organizational structure?
- What are your job responsibilities and job description?
- How integral are the mission, values, and vision statements to the studio?
- What does a typical day look like?
- What projects are you working on right now?

### Personal Experiences and Insights

- What are the toughest challenges you face day to day?
- What are the most satisfying aspects of your job?
- What do you like least about your work? Is that a consistent struggle in the field?
- What is it like working at the studio?

### Industry Thoughts

- What is your opinion on the most exciting developments in the industry right now?
- What are the future needs of the organization?
- Is the industry and the organization growing or shrinking?

### Desired Skills

**Technical skills (education and experience).**

- What educational programs or certificates do you recommend as preparation?
- What steps are necessary to break into the field?
- Are there keywords that are good to have on a resume?
- What types of technologies and software are used?

**Soft skills.**

- What is the work setting like and what does it entail?
- What other professional interactions are involved?
- What are the most important soft skills for the organization?

**Conceptual skills.**

- What skills and experience might I acquire to become more marketable to the organization?
- What are the most important character qualities for success at the studio?
- How can I demonstrate creative problem solving and big-picture thinking to the organization?

### Practical and Functional Considerations

- What entry-level positions should I be considering?
- How much experience is required?
- What are the typical salary ranges?

### Closing

- What would have been helpful information for you when you started?
- Are there any important questions I did not ask that perhaps I should have?
- Is there anyone else who would be helpful for me to talk with?

## Conclusion

Learning about and conducting the informational interview was extremely useful. I gained knowledge in areas I had not expected, and my assumptions and outdated information about parts of the organization and the vocation were corrected. That allowed my perspectives to mature, and in some respects to substantially adjust my personal development plan. Understanding the organizational structure and culture has prompted me to study management systems more closely. I also learned that it would be ideal to begin applying for positions roughly six months before graduation. It was inferred from the conversation that conflict-resolution style and high emotional intelligence are paramount for success in a project manager or producer role at the studio.

Even though I considered the interviewee a friend and networking contact before, by re-engaging him for structured information I found that his interest in my career path was refreshed as well. The conversation opened space for him to ask follow-up questions and to learn more about my current skills, knowledge, and perspectives. The experience increased my understanding of career possibilities, helped me gauge the desire and viability of pursuing them, and increased my awareness of employment and career opportunities.
