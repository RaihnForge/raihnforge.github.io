## Introduction

Utilizing the knowledge gained through my experiences and academic endeavors thus far, I will conduct a leadership planning document in accordance with guidelines that are in keeping with the Four-Step Process in Leadership Planning. These include self-assessment, application of self-assessment, goal setting, and reflection (Portolese et al., 2018). These valuations will provide me clarity in personal growth, career pursuits, and application of leadership in order to affect positive change to individuals and community.

## Leadership Philosophy

This section establishes organizational goals and leadership principles that will help assist my focus and conduct moving forward. I wish to continually re-evaluate and iterate on my leadership goals, values, and principles for as long as I am able.

### Organization Goals

My goals for my organization are to extend my expertise and skills in order to honor and enrich those around me. I wish to be a person who continually provides any small or large assistance that may inspire hope in themselves and others. I wish to be courageous and ethical in my approach, and to wholistically and authentically engage with individuals and community. I am hoping to provide support for my fellow Veterans and make real changes in their lives that allow them to shine even brighter for their families, themselves, and the communities they touch.

### Leadership Principles

**Lead with Competence.** Be informed while providing care, vision, and influence to motivate and orient a group to a common task and purpose.

**Trust and Communication are Paramount.** Be authentic and empathic, garnering trust and a caring atmosphere. Trust engenders honest and frequent communication to stay in touch with the health and needs of the organization.

**Provide Clear Vision.** Develop and commit to clear and often-communicated vision, mission, and values, the ethical foundations that relate to follower values in order to achieve organizational alignment and purpose.

**Be Aware of Power.** Understand elements of power, personal and positional, where it is and is lacking, and how and where it is being wielded to affect others.

**Integrity in All Things.** Successful teams must establish trust, harmony, commitment, accountability, and team focus. Modeling integrity provides the necessary foundation for these aspects.

**Integrate Leadership Approach.** Utilize power-influence and leverage situational approach aspects by understanding personal role and the need for adaptation based on the three Key Variables in Leadership.

**Life-Long Learner.** Ensure that referent power is managed and expert power is pursued, lest the negative aspects of charismatic leadership arise. Always understand what personal biases you may be bringing to the situation.

**Value Others.** Be diligent about respecting and empathizing with the individuals that comprise a group. Their rights, skills, values, and opinions, similar or different, are at least as valuable as your own, if not more.

**Commit to Ethics.** Maintain a strong foundation of personal values that are oriented to the consideration of societal issues. Avoid rationalizations, as they often are an indicator of unethical conduct.

**Enrich Others.** Model positive performance and accountability, while helping others achieve the best in themselves as well. The greatest gift is to assist another in finding the better parts of themselves.

### Summary

Being an authentic and empathetic individual driven by ethics and values for the greater good of the community are my aspirations. I wish to utilize my skills, personality traits, and knowledge to the betterment of the world around me. To do this I must bear in mind that trust, harmony, commitment, accountability, and team focus are key to the success of any group, and my personal contributions should be considered and improved. It is my responsibility to improve the wonderous world I find myself in.
