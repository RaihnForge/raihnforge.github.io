## Introduction

The Nurse Workstation Refresh Phase 1 is a technology refresh that is necessary to maintain high quality standards, reduce future maintenance costs, increase security, and increase efficiency for the stakeholders of Central Medical Center. Conducting Phase 1 of the greater initiative will begin the facility-wide refresh, prioritizing increased quality to areas that represent our first-line interaction with clients. 101 nurse workstations on floors 3, 4, and 5 will be updated to the more current operating system, Windows 10. Included in this document are details in support of a clear understanding of constraints for Nurse Workstation Refresh Phase 1. Costs, timelines, and scope are crucial elements of achieving project success. The clarity in their concept, execution, and control are a priority and this document communicates the understanding of each within the organization.

## Project Management Approach

Organizational standards in compliance with organizational core values for floors 3 to 5 of the Central Medical Center require that the Nurse Workstation Refresh Phase 1 project updates facilities workstations and technology. The four-stage Phase 1 project will bring the organization in line with internal and external industry standards and expectations while saving on costly maintenance and potential failure expenditures.

The project will be completed in four stages led by Joshua Keyes (Project Manager), directed by Dr. Phylicia Patterson (Project Sponsor) and supported by the Nursing Coordinator, IT Coordinator, the Master Load Team, the Install Team, IT Assists, and the Clean-up Team. All teams will report to Project Manager Keyes directly, and the staff of teams will report to team leaders. Project Sponsor Dr. Patterson will have executive authority over all project staff.

Project Manager Keyes will coordinate all aspects with support from staff and Project Sponsor Dr. Patterson. Budgetary, timeline, and scope changes must be approved by Project Manager Keyes and authorized by Project Sponsor Dr. Patterson.

## Project Scope

**In scope.** Phase 1 of the Nurse Workstation Refresh Initiative will be the complete updating of the operating system on all 101 systems that currently service floors 3 to 5 of Central Medical Center to Windows 10. The refresh will include validation testing for assumed application ecosystem, established network communication, and cyber security standards. This process will require the expenditure of not more than $500,000. Time to completion will be no longer than 6 months from initiation. Equipment will be focused on currently available on-site utilities. Staffing will include the Project Manager, Project Sponsor, Nursing Coordinator, IT Coordinator, the Master Load Team, the Install Team, IT Assists, and the Clean-up Team. All directives will consider the input of stakeholders and evaluation of Project Sponsor Dr. Patterson.

**Out of scope.** The project must not prioritize other phases of the overall initiative over the scope of Phase 1. The project is not responsible for the budget, timeline, or quality of further progression of the overall initiative. There is time set aside for phase integration by other means and arbitration should be focused during its proper time. Network infrastructure, software licensing deals, client capacity considerations, and hardware upgrade concerns are not within the scope of this project. Being a part of a larger initiative, updating a digital ecosystem in a networked infrastructure for a large organization can lead to the desire to add tasks and updates to the priority of an operating system update project. These other considerations must be addressed in follow-up projects and cannot creep into the scope of this project. Take note of possible suggestions when pertinent, and maintain trust that further improvements will be addressed in future projects and discussions.

## Milestone List

After the refresh for each floor, all floors' digital ecosystem will be evaluated and approved. The process will be validated for compliance and reliability in Stage C with thorough stress testing by IT and Cyber Security departments. ## Schedule Baseline and Work Breakdown Structure

It is important to maintain well-defined, reviewed, and tracked work packages for the Technology Refresh project. The Work Breakdown Structure for this project is managed and developed through Microsoft Project. The file is available as an attachment to this document. The file and report represent and track the Schedule Baseline for the project. The Project Manager will control the WBS and schedule baseline, issuing reports and submitting approvals as noted and requested. Reports and updates may be requested by the Project Sponsor as well as approved Project Stakeholders for review.

The WBS dictionary structure is summarized below.

- 1.1 Project Initiation, with deliverables for Project Presentation and Project Charter
- 1.2 Project Planning, with deliverables for Project Management Plan and Work Breakdown Structure
- 1.3 Execution, broken into Stage A (Master Load Development), Stage B (per-floor execution for floors 3, 4, 5), Stage C (Validation), and Stage D (Final Adjustments)
- 1.4 Control, covering Project Management, Status Meetings, Risk Management, Scope Control, and Project Management Plan updates
- 1.5 Closeout, covering procurement audit, lessons learned, file updates, formal deliverables acceptance, and project success assessment

Each Stage B floor work package decomposes to laptop and desktop processing batches (carts 1-10 / 11-20, desks 1-8 / 9-16, etc.), followed by ecosystem testing and a per-floor verified deliverable.

## Change Management Plan

Changes are likely to occur during the process of the Nurse Station Technology Refresh Phase 1. Ensuring the focus on primary project constraints such as scope, budget, and schedule will be particularly important. This project will implement a Change Control Board (CCB) as the method of reviewing and tracking petitions and approval of change. Authority, responsibility, and awareness for changes will be outlined in this section.

Approach to change should include flexibility, responsibility, and communication within our organizational culture of change. Bottom-up understanding of project status and needs will be required to gain accurate understanding and advisement for change. Priorities of change will be as follows. First, changes are within scope, time, and budgetary constraints. Second, change applications must be paramount and carefully considered before execution. Third, execution of change will be carefully controlled.

Any petition for action that may impact the three major constraints of the project must be considered a change. Documentation and communication of changes will be required before implementation. Changes are the responsibility of the Project Manager through the approved authorization of the Project Sponsor.

The CCB will be consulted with any change impacting the project and the three identified constraints. Change requests will be the responsibility of Project Manager Keyes to log and report to the CCB. The CCB will meet bimonthly in order to review change petitions. Approved changes will be implemented and documented accordingly by the Project Manager. Updated documentation will be made available to the Project Sponsor, the CCB, and select Stakeholders by the approval of the Project Sponsor.

**Roles and responsibilities.** Project Sponsor approves and authorizes all changes impacting the three constraints. Project Manager manages petitions for change, analyzes potential impacts, mediates and gains understanding of proposed changes, documents change approvals, communicates changes to approved parties, and sits on the CCB. Project Team and Stakeholders submit change request forms, which must be filled out as completely as possible to ensure proper consideration.

## Cost Management Plan

The cost management of the project will be the responsibility of Project Manager Keyes and will be authorized by Project Sponsor Dr. Patterson. Meetings between the Project Sponsor and Project Manager will occur bi-monthly, with preparations and calculations of the budget being made by the Project Manager. Any deviations beyond anticipated variances from baseline will be the responsibility of the Project Manager under the authority of the Project Sponsor.

Control Accounts will be created for each work package of the WBS at the fifth level. This level is chosen based on the majority of project install materials being broken down into the fifth level of the WBS. Control Accounts will be given 100 percent credit at the beginning of their third-level phase to ensure readiness of resources. Costs may be rounded to the nearest 100th dollar and 8th hour (1 day) of work. This option has been chosen to mitigate time investment on preparation of budget evaluations due to the frequency of updating and auditing.

Cost variances of +/- 0.2 will be indicated at yellow for cautionary, and a +/- 0.5 will be given to indexes that are considered out of anticipated budget. These alert-level variances will be reported to the Project Sponsor and will be the responsibility to validate and control by the Project Manager. Final changes to the budget are prepared by the Project Manager and approved through the authority of the Project Sponsor.

**Measuring project costs.** Schedule Variance (SV), Cost Variance (CV), Schedule Performance Index (SPI), and Cost Performance Index (CPI) will be the primary means of calculating costs for the project. Variances between the SPI or CPI above 0.2 will require reporting by the Project Manager to the Project Sponsor. SPI or CPI above a 0.5 will require reporting as well as corrective planning by the Project Manager and approval by the Project Sponsor.

Reporting format will be the utilization of MS Project as well as written recommendations, calculations, and status updates by the Project Manager. An accurate Estimate at Completion (EAC) and Estimate to Completion (ETC) earned-value analysis are expected to be reported.

**Project budget.** The Project Budget has been delineated under fixed costs, material costs, labor costs, and a reserve, to fall within the mandated $500,000 budget. Fixed costs account for usage of building resources to perform and displace current operations. Material costs account for computer workstation replacement hardware. Labor costs are calculated utilizing assigned resources to work packages via MS Project. The reserve makes up the remaining cost difference to the mandated $500,000. Technology projects tend toward significant variances. Unforeseen circumstances may allow for the request by the Project Manager of reserve funds that will be approved for use under the authorization of the Project Sponsor. Currently the project is projected to complete under the mandated budget.

## Project Scope Management Plan

The Project Manager has the authority and responsibility for scope management. The Scope Statement, WBS, WBS Dictionary, and Statement of Work will be the responsibility of the Project Manager. The measure and verification of scope will be subject to Quality Checklists, Scope Baseline, and Work Performance Measurements reviewed by the Project Manager and approved by the Project Sponsor.

Initiation of the scope change process may be conducted by the Project Sponsor or Project Manager. Other staff members and stakeholders may petition for changes through established organizational structure and approved communication forums. Changes may be made to the scope only by the Project Manager, with the authorization of the Project Sponsor. Communication and documentation of any changes to scope to affiliated stakeholders and team members will be the responsibility of the Project Manager. The accepting of final project deliverables and approval of accepted project scope is the responsibility of the Project Sponsor.

## Schedule Management Plan

The Schedule Management Plan will provide detail on the approach, control, resources, and adjustments of the schedule. Scheduling will align with the predictive approach. The Precedence Diagram Method (PDM) will be utilized. Critical Path Methodology will be employed to derive and determine accurate and adaptable schedule management. Focus and management of the critical path will allow the team to prioritize the completion date of the project. By ensuring that critical paths are attended to appropriately, delays to project completion will be mitigated or removed.

The generation of the schedule will account for resources as well as durations and dependencies of work packages. It will be managed by the scope statement, WBS, WBS Dictionary, completion of Baseline Schedule, Final Budget Approval, Project Start Date, Project Implementation approval, and confirmation of final deliverables. The software utilized to manage the schedule will be Microsoft Project 2016.

The Project Manager will report weekly updates of schedule progress to the Project Sponsor. Any unforeseen delays or adjustments to the critical path will be immediately reported to the Project Sponsor. Additional resources or significant re-assignments of project resources may be proposed by the Project Manager and authorized by the Project Sponsor.

Petitions for changes to schedule that will create a slippage, duration, or lag of 8 percent or greater as compared to the Schedule Baseline will require the express approval of the Project Sponsor. Changes below this threshold may be approved by the Project Manager.

In the event that a change in scope is necessary and approved, the Project Manager may request a re-baselined project schedule. A scope change or re-baseline of the project schedule may only be conducted with the approval of both Project Manager and Project Sponsor. Recording changes in scope and the updating of the scope change log is the responsibility of the Project Manager. Communication of changes will be the responsibility of the Project Manager.
