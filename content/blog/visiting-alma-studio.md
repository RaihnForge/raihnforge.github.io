I spent the day at the [Alma](https://getalma.com) studio, and I left with the kind of energy you only get from being in a room full of people who actually care about what they're building.

## The Narwhals in the Waiting Room

The first thing you notice walking in is that the waiting room is full of cute little narwhals. I love it. The narwhal is one of those creatures that *looks* mythical: a fat happy dolphin with a unicorn horn. You'd swear it had to be made up. But it's a real animal that genuinely exists in the world.

That's a perfect symbol for Alma, and I don't think it's an accident. Their tagline is **"Be so amazing that people think you are a myth."** A school technology company that genuinely cares about teachers and students sounds like a thing somebody made up to sell a slide. Alma is the one that turns out to actually be real.

## What Alma Does

Alma is a cloud-based Student Information System, the spine of school operations. Student records, attendance, scheduling, traditional and competency-based grading, state reporting, fee management, communication between teachers, students, and families. The category sounds dry until you remember that every one of those workflows is something a real educator, family, or student has to live with every day. Bad software here means a teacher loses an hour they could have spent with a kid. Good software means the opposite.

What stands out about Alma is who it's for and who it's by. They serve a wide spread of institutions: state agencies, large and small districts, independent and charter schools, international schools, school networks. They describe themselves as "built for educators, by educators, and loved by communities." That phrase reads like marketing until you meet the team and realize they mean it.

## What the Studio Felt Like

You can tell a lot about a company by walking through its studio. Alma's was full of the coolest, most passionate people I've met in a long time. Engineers, designers, support folks, and product people who could explain, in detail and without performative jargon, exactly which problem each part of the system is solving for which type of school.

The thing that hit me hardest was how clearly the team holds the *student* at the center. SIS development is the kind of work that easily drifts into spreadsheets and state reports, but every conversation I overheard kept circling back to the same question: does this make the day better for a teacher, a family, a kid? When that's the actual posture of the team, the work shows it.

It's also a properly small team for the scale of what they're doing, which is the way I like it. Small teams who care more than they have to are the ones that ship the work that lasts.

## The Sticker

I made the team a sticker. A friendly little narwhal alongside the Alma mark. "Norwahl," because the swag deserves a pun, and because the narwhal already does most of the work of saying what this company is.

## Why It Matters

Education software is one of the most consequential design domains there is, and most of it is terrible. Teachers know it. Families know it. Students know it. Alma is the rare team where the people building the SIS clearly *like* educators and *like* the problem, and you can feel that in both the product and the room.

The mythical company that actually cares. Turns out it's real. It just has a horn on its head.

If you work in or around a school and you've never looked at them, [getalma.com](https://getalma.com) is worth the time.
