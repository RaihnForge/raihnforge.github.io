Mecromage is a 2D metalvania set in a world of dark mechanical fantasy. You play Kochay, a Frostwolf cleric and half arcane construct, following a trail of corruption that leads to her father — the Mecromage Tyrant. The game began in 2009 at the indie studio I co-founded, survived that studio's closure, and is currently being rebuilt on an AI-assisted Phaser 4 pipeline through Archkey Studio.

My role spans the full creative stack: producer, game director, artist, and software designer.

## World

One floating island crowned by the Mecropolis — the Mecromage's castle. Four zones, each with a distinct pillar: exploration, traversal, combat, and puzzle. The visual language draws from the dark industrial-gothic tradition: rusted machinery embedded in stone ruins, frost-glazed corridors, creatures that blur the line between built and born.

## Character Design

Kochay is a Frostwolf cleric and half arcane construct trained in frost magic and acrobatic combat. Her moveset is built around a core loop: freeze → grapple → shatter. Every enemy, surface, and environmental object is either a Focal Object (permanent, indestructible) or a host that can be frozen into a temporary Modified Object — which then becomes grappleable, climbable, or throwable.

## Production Arc

The project spans three chapters: Unchosen Paths (2009–2018), the dormant years (2018–2023), and the current Archkey Studio rebuild (2024–Present). The postmortem of the original studio failure — ambition outran velocity, tooling of the era couldn't close the loop — informs the production model of the rebuild: AI-assisted pipelines, time-boxed studies, and explicit project-vs-process separation.

The game is in active development. A playable vertical slice is the next milestone.
