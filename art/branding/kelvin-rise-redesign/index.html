Kelvin RISE Microlearning — Content Redesign

Medium
UX / Content Design
Year
2025

Iterative redesign of Kelvin Education's RISE microlearning card sequence. Four prototype densities tested before landing a 'Combined Ideas' rough draft that keeps the introduction, video pacing, and the interaction beat without dragging the learner through redundant cards.

Iterative content-structure redesign for Kelvin Education’s RISE microlearning sequence. The brief: a single lesson was running eleven content cards in the prototype version, which was more friction than the audience (kids) could carry, and the pacing felt diffuse. The work walks four density variations side by side — Prototype (11 cards), Efficient (7 cards), Adjust Initial (5 cards), and Adjust Merge (6 cards) — and lands on a final “Combined Ideas” rough draft that keeps the moments that earn their place and drops the ones that don’t.

The chosen Adjust Merge structure runs six cards: Introduction, Video Start, Interaction, Middle Video, Process: Stepped Process, and Video End. Each card has a clear job (welcome / behavioral demo / question-and-answer beat / instructional video / hands-on process / conclusion) and a deliberate “next steps” affordance (press play, down arrow, salutation) so a young learner always knows what to do next without a teacher narrating over the screen.

Beyond the card-count exercise, the deck walks the visual treatment for each card type — the play-button affordance, the title-card pacing for video, the question-encouragement card, the labeled-graphic interaction beat, and the salutation handoff at the end. Character art and color system are Kelvin’s existing language; the contribution is the content architecture and the per-card decisions that make the sequence land for a young audience.
