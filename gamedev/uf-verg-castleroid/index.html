A viability exercise in retro graphics — recreating and extending the tile-based visual language of classic Castlevania.

The Castleroid project is a deliberate study in retro tile-based graphics. The goal: understand the visual language of classic Castlevania at a deep level by recreating and then extending it.

Why This Exercise

The Castlevania series solved a hard problem — how to build a sense of place, dread, and wonder from a 16×16 tile grid with a handful of colors. Understanding how that works requires doing it, not just looking at it.

Every tile is a decision: about silhouette, about value contrast, about what repeats and what varies. Working through this by hand builds intuition that reading about pixel art never can.

VERG Framework

VERG (Viability Exercise in Retro Graphics) is the internal framework for these studies. The Castleroid episodes are the first major application of it. The work feeds directly into Mecromage and other game projects.

Episodes

  • Castleroid: 01 — Initial tileset recreation based on Castlevania 1
  • Castleroid: 02 — Extended environmental depth, animated elements
