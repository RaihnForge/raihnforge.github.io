Indie game studio co-founded in 2009. Home of Mecromage and multiple unreleased projects spanning nearly a decade of development.

Unchosen Paths was an indie game studio I co-founded in 2009 with a guitarist known for Castlevania remixes. The name reflects the creative philosophy: taking roads that aren’t mapped out yet.

My Role

I served as producer, artist, game director, and software designer. That range wasn’t by choice so much as by necessity — small studios require everyone to do everything. But it was the best possible education in what it actually takes to make a game.

My core responsibilities were conceptual artwork and overall project vision. I was the one who decided what the game felt like before anyone wrote a line of code.

Projects

The studio’s primary project was Mecromage — a side-scrolling Metroidvania that absorbed the majority of our creative energy from 2013 onward. Alongside it were several exploratory concepts documented in the workshop, including environment design exercises, character concepting, and game system prototypes.

Timeline

2009–2018. Nine years of iterative work, collaborative growth, and lessons learned the hard way.

The studio formally wound down in 2018, but Mecromage continues under my solo development.
