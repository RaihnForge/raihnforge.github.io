The Concept

Necromancer, meet Mech.

Mecromage lives at the seam between the mechanical and the arcane. Heavy armor and industrial ruins on one side. Ice, mana, and ritual on the other. Creatures that blur the line between built and born — dark fantasy with a machine edge.

The design philosophy draws on four classics: Final Fantasy I, Castlevania, Zelda, and Doom. To that lineage it adds frost magic, a grappling hook, dynamic environmental interactions, and a metal soundtrack. The art direction and the mood have been intact for over a decade. What's new is a production pipeline finally capable of carrying it across the finish line.

The Heroine

Kochay,
the Frostwolf Cleric

A Frostwolf cleric and half arcane construct, trained in frost magic and acrobatic combat. She wakes in a ruined cave with no memory of how she arrived, then steps into a realm she once knew but no longer recognizes.

The story unfolds from that first breath — what became of her, of her order, of her realm, and gradually, of her father, the Mecromage Tyrant, whose corruption is the central mystery she follows.

She fights with a set of cleric relics: a shield, a chain mace, a grappling hook, and her frost magic. Acrobatic movement lets her read each room and decide how to reshape it.

Core Mechanic

Freeze. Grapple. Shatter.

Traversal should feel free, and the world should feel reactive. Every room is a sandbox of state changes — traversal becomes a conversation with the environment rather than a sequence of jumps.

01 — CHANGE STATE

❄ Freeze

Frost magic deals no damage. It changes state. Freeze an enemy or surface and it converts into a grappleable object — feeding the loop.

02 — THE VERB

⛓ Grapple

Thrown in any direction, the chain engages whatever it touches — a Focal Object. Pull, swing, climb, or sling. The whole game is conjugated around this verb.

03 — PAYOFF

⚒ Shatter

A frozen host becomes a Modified Object: temporary and shatterable. Break it with a Shield Bash, a Pip 3 strike, or a high-speed impact — some deal area damage.

The Freezing System · Three States

Frost is temporary, and everything thaws. Kochay can also reverse a Modified Object back to its host on demand with a fire utility — keeping every tactical choice grounded in real time.

Thawed

The default. Every enemy, every pool of water, every host object starts here.

Frosted

First hit of frost. The target slows and gradually thaws back. A second hit pushes it further.

Frozen

Fully stopped. The host becomes a Modified Object: grappleable and shatterable until it thaws.

Focal Objects · Every Frozen Thing Becomes a Tool

A Focal Object exists in two forms — a permanent Primary (indestructible alloy, often embedded in walls) and a temporary Modified version that emerges when Kochay freezes a living host. Each form has its own silhouette, and its own verb.

Anchor PointCoffin or crystal shape. Pull to it, then drop. Host: medium enemies — falls and shatters on impact for area damage.
Through PointStabby cross. Pull toward and through it, flying out the far side. Host: the Mana Vortex.
Grapple StripA row of teeth. Pull to it and climb. Host: water — freeze deep water and its surface becomes a climbable ice wall.
BoulderSmall sphere. Sling it into enemies or surfaces to shatter. Host: small enemies such as Flyers and Crawlers.
Swing PointHorseshoe. Swing across hazards, then release with jump. Host: ceiling hangers — Spawners and Hanging Bats.
The Moveset

A Focused Arsenal

A purposeful weapon set, instead of a bloated arsenal. Every tool conjugates with the grapple verb and the freezing system.

Chain Mace

Repeated slices build Pips on a 0–3 scale, extending range then power. Charge attacks branch by direction: Uppercut, Chop, Lariat, 360 Twirl.

Shield

Block in any direction, even airborne. Shield + Attack fires the Freeze Shot. Run + Shield triggers the Shield Bash.

Grapple

Thrown omnidirectionally — the hub verb of the moveset. Upgrades add damage while grappling and area damage at the endpoint.

Pip System

A momentum economy. Consecutive strikes earn pips; pausing loses them. Pip 3 unlocks shatter-grade power.

Health & Alloy

Three hearts. Collect Alloy — 30 forms a metallic shield icon that absorbs one hit, capped at heart capacity.

Wolf Mode

Max your armor patches and become a steel wolf for 20–30 seconds: melee that petrifies, and a grapple that becomes a grab-and-throw.

Mobility

Run for higher jumps, Slide, Backflip, Kip (omnidirectional air dash), Shield Surf on water, and Ground Slam.

Frost Magic

The change agent. No damage — state. The engine that turns every host in the world into a temporary tool.

The World

One Island. Four Pillars.

A single floating island, crowned by the Mecromage's castle — the Mecropolis. A Central Hub with altars to save and teleport, two wilderness paths leading outward, dungeons holding colored keys, and the final reckoning past the Reaper.

Four Pillars of Fun

Every component of Mecromage serves one or more pillars. Each level type answers to a single primary pillar — drawn structurally from Final Fantasy I's mythic scale, Castlevania II's hub-and-return, Zelda's keyed dungeons, and Doom's color-coded legibility.

Combat

Read the enemy type and choose the optimal technique. Many encounters have a right answer.

Exploration

Out from the hub into unmapped wilderness, where the Sage and the secrets reward the curious.

Traversal

Grapple, swing, climb, slide. Movement itself is a source of joy and challenge.

Puzzle

Dungeons as locks. Frost and grapple interactions become spatial puzzles.

Environment Design

Ruins That Read at a Glance

The environment system is built around tile-silhouette language — every surface type reads differently at a glance, communicating traversal options before the player consciously registers them.

Rusted machinery embedded in stone ruins. Frost-glazed corridors. The seam between mechanical and arcane runs through every plate.

The Arc · 2009 → Present

Sixteen Years in the Forge

The longest creative thread in the portfolio. A studio that built beautifully but couldn't ship. A stretch of dormant years. A rebuild on new terms.

Chapter One
2009–2018

Unchosen Paths

A two-person studio co-founded with a guitarist known for Castlevania remixes. The name captured the philosophy — take roads not yet mapped. Producer, artist, game director, software designer, all at once. Mecromage became the flagship, but the studio wound down in 2018 without shipping.

Chapter Two
2018–2023

The Dormant Years

Work continued, but only sporadically and only solo. The tooling of the era hadn't closed the velocity gap, and the project remained too large for one person at the old pace. The vision was too strong to abandon — so the work waited while the lessons, and the tools, compounded.

Chapter Three
2024–Present

Archkey Studio & the AI-Assisted Pipeline

The path forward isn't a new engine or art style — it's a new production model. Archkey Studio is a learning-first studio built around AI-assisted development, with Mecromage as its reason to exist. Foundational assets are being ported forward; a playable vertical slice is the next milestone. For the first time, the math on getting there closes.

Why It Didn't Ship — and Why It Will Now

The original failure was never about craft. The concept art, sprites, and environments still look right a decade later, and the engineering — UI resolution, dynamic 2D platforms, tile systems — was real. What broke was velocity: a metalvania's scope is enormous, every asset hand-made and every system hand-coded, with no force multipliers. The math never closed.

The rebuild changes that math. Code generation, AI-assisted art workflows, and rapid Phaser 4 prototyping collapse the hours-to-output ratio — a solo developer with AI leverage operating near small-team velocity. The craft stays human; the drafts and boilerplate don't. For the first time in the project's history, the path to a playable vertical slice closes.
