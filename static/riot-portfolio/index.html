How I think about visual systems

The work isn't the deliverable. It's the system that makes the next deliverable easier to ship on-brand. I design once, then design the rules that let the next 50 versions be handled by staff, by partners, by anyone — and still come back recognizably us.

That shows up here as overlays tuned against in-game UI, social templates the team uses without me, cheermote families with shared pose-and-palette grammar, and tile systems that hold up at gameplay scale. The throughline is restraint and consistency, not volume.
